description: "From Fort Fidius, Seagrove laments that his failed efforts at achieving peace with the Creek Nation are entirely owing to the unruly conduct of the people of Georgia. Reports that there are\
  \ two expeditions on foot against the Creek towns; one party from Green County and other from Washington. Suggests that Georgia Governor Telfair is encouraging this activity. \r\nExpresses hope that the\
  \ Oakfuskees, who wrongly incurred the white man's wrath instead of the culprits, the horse stealing Cowetas, will exact revenge on the Cowetas. Seagrove fears that the conduct of the white people in\
  \ this country will destroy all hopes of peace; refers to the malignant breath of anti federalists and land speculators who undermine the peace. Suggests that if Barnard can give favorable accounts, Seagrove\
  \ will again attempt to venture out to meet with Creek leadership."
