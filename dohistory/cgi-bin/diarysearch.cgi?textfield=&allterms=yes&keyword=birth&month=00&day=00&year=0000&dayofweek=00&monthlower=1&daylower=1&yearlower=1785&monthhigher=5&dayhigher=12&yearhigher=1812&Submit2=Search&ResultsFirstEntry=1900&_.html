 February 10, 1800, Monday

view full Diary page

 

Birth John Chamberlins Dagt.

Clear but not So Cold. I have been at Chamberlins, his wife was Safe Deld at near midnight of a daughter. Old mrss Savage and her Son Jamess wife were Calld there. the Old Lady and my Self tarried till morn.   at Chamberlins. Birth 7th. 
 

 

 February 12, 1800, Wednesday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear morn, Cloudy afternoon. James Page, his wife & Childn Calld here, Doct Cony and mrss Fisher allso. mr Ballard gone to Survey the Jaill yard. I was invited to attend at mr Bunkers weding to mrs Fanny Cocx. Son Jona & wife went. Hepsy tarried with their Childn.    
 

 

 February 13, 1800, Thursday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear but Cold. I went this morn to See Hannahs foot, find it Easier but Shee Cannot walk. I went aftern to See Esqr warrin, find him very low. I Calld at mr Millers, Herrimans and Dind at mr Burtuns. Came home before Sun Sett.   at mr Blacks. find Esqr warrin very low.  
 

 

 February 18, 1800, Tuesday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear and very Pleast. mr Ballard, my Self, Cyrus, Jona, his wife and 2 oldest sons attended at the meeting house. heard prayers at the funeral of Georg Warrin Esqr. he was interd in the masonic mode, a Number of the Brithren of that Society were present. I Calld and visited mrss Crage foren.   at mr Crages & at funeral of Esqr Warrin.  
 

 

 February 22, 1800, Saturday

view full Diary page

 

 this day, by recomendation of the President of the United States, was set apart to Commemorate the Death of Genl Georg Washington. we met at the meeting hous in Augusta where there were assembled the Lodg of Hallowell. Capt Casts Company of malitia and a larg concoarce of people. a prayer Deld by ye Revd mr Stone, an oration by mr Whitwell and a Closing prayer by mr Gillet.   at the meeting house on the Death of Genl Georg Washington, ye aniversary of his Death.  
 

 

 February 23, 1800, Sunday

view full Diary page

 

 Snowd. mr Ballard to meeting, Son Cyrus & Hepsy to Son Pollards. I Sent Each of his Childn a pair of wolen hoes. Dagt Ballard Calld in here, informs me her Dagt is unwell.   at home.  
 

 

 March 1, 1800, Saturday

view full Diary page

 

 A very Severe Snow Storm. I feel my Self very unwell, went onto ye bed but was Calld to rise and go to Son Lambards to See Son Pollards infant, find it very Sick. we gave it Senna and Rhubarb. my Son went home to take Care of his famely.   at Son Lambds. Harry Pollard very Sick there.  
 

 

 March 3, 1800, Monday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear. the Babe is no better. Son Pollard Lambd went for Doct Cony to Come and See the babe, he was absent. Son Pollard Came and Calld Doct Colman who recommended Castor Oil; we made use of it. Cony Came at Evng, he gave it as his opineon it was Dangerously ill, advised to pursue the Cast Oil which we Did. it was very Sick thro the night. the Doct left Snake root, Cammomile and an other ingreedient and Parrigoric which was used acording to his direction to a punctilio but to no affect, the illness still increases.   at Ditoes. mr Stone there on a visit. the babe very Sick.  
 

 

 March 4, 1800, Tuesday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear. I am with my Childn yet. Cony Came and gave the babe an Emetic which had a kind operation but, alas, the Cause is not yet removed. he gave it Excesive washings with Brandy and red oinions applyd to the belly and feet. we followed his Directions. mrss Haywood and Polly Pollard Sett up with the Child, it was thot to be Expireing at 4h morn.   at Ditoes. the babe is no Better.  
 

 

 March 9, 1800, Sunday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear and not so Cold. Son & Dagt Pollard, with their two oldest Childn, Calld here ys morn, Fanny Hodges with them. mr Ballard, Cyrus and my Self, Jona, his wife and Lefaett went to meeting. Revd mr Stone Discorsed from psalm 19, 7 and 8 v. Dagt Ballard and I Calld to See Abigail Andrews who has pased thee operation of Taping for the Dropsy, find her Comfortable. after take Some refreshment, my Dagt went to visit Neigr Parkers famely who are Sick. find Sally very unwell. they put a blister on her side acording to the Docts direction.   at meeting, at mr Andress and at mr Parker. at Son Lambds at the intermition.  
 

 

 March 15, 1800, Saturday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear. I have been at home. Dagt Ballard and three youngest Childn here. Shee is not so well as Could be wisht. I have knitt two pair hoes and pair Socks and futed one pair hoes sinc I left home on Tuesday.   at home. Dagt Ballard here. John Shaws wf Deld of a Dagt by mrs [      ].  
 

 

 March 22, 1800, Saturday

view full Diary page

 

Birth Robinsons Dagt.

 A Severe Snow Storm. I was Calld to See mrss Robinson who was in Labour. Shee Continued ill till 6h Evn when Shee was Safe Deld of her 3d Child and 2nd Daugt. I tarried all night, Sett up with the patient. Shee is Comfortable as Could be Expected. I finisht Jack and Le faetts Stockins.   at mr Robinson. Birth 8th. recd 9/.  
 

 

 March 23, 1800, Sunday

view full Diary page

 

 Stormd all Day. I tarried with my Patient till near night when mr Robinson Conducted me home. the Snow is Drifted So that it was with difuculty w got thro. Son & dagt Ballard and Ephm, yr little Son, Supt here. mr Robinson paid me 6/.   at Ditoes.  
 

 

 March 24, 1800, Monday

view full Diary page

 

 Stormy, Snowd the most of ye Day. Cleard of at 8 Evn. Son & Dagt Lambard, with their two Sons, Calld here at 11h foren on their return from Son Pollards. they are all well. Son Pollard gone to See his Father. mr Ballard got a load of wood. Son Jona been to Sydna, he broatt me _ Busl Rie which Dagt Pollard sent me as a present. I have been kniting and Sewing, workt in the Sellar Some.   at home.  
 

 

 March 31, 1800, Monday

view full Diary page

 

Birth Joseph Browns 6th Dagt.

Cloudy and Clear. I was Calld at 4h 30 [morn] to go to Joseph Browns wife, arivd there at 6. Shee was Safe Deld at 8h 15m of her 6th Dagt. I tarried with her till 3h pm, arivd at home at 5. I left her and inft as well as Could be Expected. Shee had a Laborious illness but Blessed be God, it terminated in Safety. may Shee and I ascribe the prais to the the [sic] Great Parent of the universe.   at Joseph Browns, Hallowel. Birth 9th. recd 6/.  
 

 

 April 2, 1800, Wednesday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear. mr Trask and wife Calld to See me. mr Bullin and wife dind here. mrs Pierce and Sabery Price took Tea. I have been at home kniting, feel more Comfortable.   at home. mr Bullin & wife here.  
 

 

 April 6, 1800, Sunday

view full Diary page

 

Birth Eliab Shaws Dagt.

raind Part of the day. mr Ballard went to meeting. I was Calld at 5h 30m pm by Eliab Shaw to go and See his wife who was in Labour. Shee was Safe Deld at 9 Evn of a fine Dagt. I left them about midnight and reacht home at 1, the wrideing is very bad.   at Eliab Shaws. Birth 10th. 
 

 

 April 13, 1800, Sunday

view full Diary page

 

 Cloudy and some rain. mr Ballard only of our famely attended public worship. mr Stones text in pSalm XIX. Son Ephm is here, Jon a and wife Supt with us. I was Calld at Evng by mr Francise to go and see the wife of Abram Devenport who was in Labour. I find, on my arival, that old mrs Devenpt, mrs Lee and wido Patin were there. mrs Shaw was afterwards Calld. the Patients illness Came on and Shee was unDer my imediate Care at 11h 50m.   at Abm Devenpts.  
 

 

 April 14, 1800, Monday

view full Diary page

 

X. Birth Abm Devenpts 3d Son. XX.

mrs Devenport was Safe Deld of her 3d Son at 1h morn and is Cleverly. mrs Shaw went home, we all took Some repose. we took Breakfast and afterwards attended prayers and I left my patients Cleverly, and reacht home at 11h 30m. I Calld at Esqr Haywoods and left yarn which mrs Patin Sent her.   at Ditoes. Birth 11th. recd 12/ as a rewd. Cyruss white faced Cow Calvd.  
 

 

 April 15, 1800, Tuesday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear. I have been at home, moovd the Banking fm ye North End of ye hous. was Calld at 6h Evn by Jessy Bullin to go and See his wife, find her poorly when I arivd at near 8h. we were up with her all night, Shee is very unwell.   at Jessy Bullins.  
 

 

 April 16, 1800, Wednesday

view full Diary page

 

X. Birth Jesy Bullins Dagt. XX.

Clear. I have been at mr Bullns, my patient very ill all day. (Shee (Blessed be the Great Parent of ye univerce) was Safe Deld at 5h 48m Pm of a Dagt, her first Born. the inft was aparently dead but by Gods Blessing it revived and is Cleverly.   at Jessy Bullins. Birth 12th. recd 12/ as a rewd.  
 

 

 April 17, 1800, Thursday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear. I left my Patients Cleverly at 8h morn and reacht home at 10h 17m. I was acompanied by Phillip Bullin who went and Conducted Becky Faught to his Brors as Nurs.   at mr Bullins and at mr Catons.  
 

 

 April 18, 1800, Friday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear and warm. I helpt remove the dirt back of our house. Doct Cony past by. mr Caton Calld me at 7h 30m Evng to go and See his wife who is unwell.   by 10h Evng.  
 

 

 April 19, 1800, Saturday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear and very Pleast. I was at Catons, my Patient about house. mr Ballard and Others out Surveying and altering road by Catons.   at Catons.  
 

 

 April 20, 1800, Sunday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear and very warm. I was at Catons. mrs Chambertin Came there and went home again. the patient Calld her women in the night.   at Ditoes.
