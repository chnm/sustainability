<p>Recently transcriber Mystakaphoros brought us a somewhat lighthearted letter detailing the ragtag conditions that soldiers and government officials often faced. Now, it isn’t the <em>conditions</em> that are funny; it’s the way the writer describes them. He describes the equipment given to the cavalry as “catchpenny;” he laments that some horses could go “scarce a mile but something broke.” And yet another horse is “is so miserably jaded with the journey thus far that I am afraid to risk him.” It is possible to sense the writer’s frustration, even while we are chuckling at his language.</p>
