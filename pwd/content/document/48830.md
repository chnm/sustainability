description: Humphreys talks to Knox about the scarphing (joints that hold two pieces of wood together) of a frigate's keel.  The scarphs were proportioned for three pieces, but because they are going to
  be in five pieces the length of the keel for the 36-gun frigates will be 146 feet, which will leave only 17 feet of solid keel between each scarph.  Humphreys discusses ways to modify this situation,
  and notes that Colonel Hacket is very knowledgeable about these issues and so will defer to him on a solution.
