 March 13, 1794, Thursday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear & very Pleast. I went to mr Burtuns, made a visit there & at mr Crages; returnd at Evng. Dolly went with others to ye hook as a Spectator to their Singing School. mrs Burtun gave me a Kenester.   at mr Burtuns & Crages. Doct Ward & Polly Bullen Married.  
 

 

 April 11, 1794, Friday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear. I have been at home. Doct Coney Calld here. mr Ballard went to ye hook. I had a Severe pain in my head at Evng & greatly opprest with wind in my Stomach. had got So much Ease as to Sleep when I was Calld by moses Springer to See his Lady.   at mr Moses Springers.  
 

 

 April 26, 1794, Saturday

view full Diary page

 

X. Birth James Kenneys Dagt. XX.

rainy. Doct Williams Dind here. mr Ballard is gone to mr Thomass, a Law Seut there between mr Peter Parker & Colo Sewall. I was Calld at 2h Evn to See James Kenneys wife who was in Labour, & was Delivd at 11h of a Dagt; her husband was absent. I tarried all night, had no Sleep.   at James Kenneys. Birth 18th. 
 

 

 May 12, 1794, Monday

view full Diary page

 

 rain ys morn, Clear Part of ye Day. mr Hodges Calld me to See his Dagt Fanny who is Labouring under ye indisposition of a Brokin Limb. I find her very restless. Doct Cony there.   at mr Hodges to See his Child.  
 

 

 May 15, 1794, Thursday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear & warm. I have workt in my gardin, planted mellons & Cucumrs. Dolly is gone to her Sisters. Ephm workt for mr Waid foren, is gone to See a phraim raisd for Matw Haywood Esqr. I wrote to Doct Barton by mr Burtun yesterday.   at home. I wrote Doct Barton yesterdy & Sent by mr Burtun.  
 

 

 May 19, 1794, Monday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear till aftern. mr Ballard at Doct Coneys. I was Calld to See Rold Smiths wife who is unwell. I Calld at mr Beamans & P. Norcross & T. Kennys. I bot an Earthen Vessel, price 1/.   at [Han] Clearks.  
 

 

 June 13, 1794, Friday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear ye most of ye Day. mr Ballard is gone to Washington to Survey for mr John Davis, who removd from hollowell to the farm which was owned by Doct Williams. I have been to mr Livermores. Sally Wilms & Polly Livrmr been takeing a puke. I receivd Letters from Brthers Collins, Moore & Barton, my friends there are well Except Doct Barton. Old Lady Town Deseast in may last.   at mr Livermors. the Girls unwell. I receivd Letters of ye 1st inst from Doc t Barton & Bror Collins. Death aunt Town may last.  
 

 

 June 30, 1794, Monday

view full Diary page

 

X. Birth Son Pollard Daugt, first Born. XX.

Clear. I was Calld at 9h morn to my Daughter Pollard who was in Labour, found her very ill. her women were Calld (mrs Pollard, Capin, Black, Hamlin & Burtun) and Shee was Safe Deliverd at 1h Pm of a Daught, her first Born, and I tarried with her allnight. Shee is Cleverly. mr Ballard went to Survey for Doct Parker.   at Son Pollards. Birth 28th. Dolly Calld to See her Sister.  
 

 

 July 3, 1794, Thursday

view full Diary page

 

 Cloudy foren. Charls Cocks Came at 11h morn for Sally to go to his Dads, his Brother Gershom is very Sick, his Life is Dispard of by his friends. I have been at work in my gardin this foren, Sett Some Cabbag & Turnips. I went to Son Pollards, find my Dagt but feeble. I was at mr Wessons Store, bot 1 lb Coffee @ 1/4, _ lb Brim stone /4, 1 oz Sinnamon /9, 7 gills Ginn 1/9, 1_ lb raisans @ 1 1/3, Snuff /2. at Doct Colmans, 2 oz Spermaseta, 2 oz manna, _ oz myr, Do alloes 1/2, Vial & Salts, Camphr _ oz.   at home. at Son Pollards & other Neighbors.  
 

 

 July 26, 1794, Saturday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear & very warm. mr Ballard went to his meddow. Son Jona is gone to winslow. I went to See mrs Parker below here, find her very feeble. Jonas wife & Childn went home by water, our boys Conducted them. Selva Moore Sleeps here. I have wrote to Bror Barton. Dolly went to See mrs Pitts, finds her fadeing.   at mr Parkers. wrole to Doct Barton. Jonas wife & Sons went home. Selva Moore Sleeps here.  
 

 

 August 1, 1794, Friday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear. we removd mrs Hamlin out of the Chamber, Shee Seems more Comfortable. I went to See mrs Pitts aftern, his mother there. I was at mr Wessons & Doct Colmans. returnd home, find my Dagt Pollard, mrs Barton and Polly Pollard here.   at Ditoes & others.  
 

 

 August 7, 1794, Thursday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear. the Boys went to fince the Stacks of hay. Sally went to See mrs Pitts, finds her more unwell. I had been to work in my Gardin & was fatagud, but went & watcht. Shee has a Disentary and much grip.   at Shubal Pittss, his wife very Sick. I watcht. wrote to Doct Barton.  
 

 

 August 9, 1794, Saturday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear foren, Cloudy aftern. I have Done Matters in my Gardin & went to the hook. Left a Letter for Doct Barton at Isaac Livermores. Calld at Capt F Browns. Sally is gone to Son Pollards, the Boys finisht reaping our wheat & bound it.   at the hook. mr Perham of Sandy [river] interd ys Day.  
 

 

 August 21, 1794, Thursday

view full Diary page

 

 rainy. I have been with mrs Pitts all Day. Shee has been more Comfortable than Shee was yesterday. Doct Williams Calld to See her. Spoke Les couraging, Said he had known Some find help from using the Bark, but Did not wish to advise to it unless Shee Chose to. Shee wisht to try it, we prepard it & Shee took 7 Tea Spoonfuls. I Saw no ill Efect from it. I left her at 6h & 30m & returnd home, find Dolly a little better than Shee was.   at mr Pittss, Doct Williams there.  
 

 

 September 3, 1794, Wednesday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear & very pleast. Dolly went to Son Pollards, Cyrus to Varsalboro. mrs Livermore was here. mr Pitts & mrs Barton Came home with Dolly. Sally Hamlin & Charls Cocks took Tea with us. mr Robbins Calld me to See his wife who is not So well as Shee Could wish. Cyrus returnd home. I wrote to Doct ["Barton." - implied], Sent it by mr Craig. he and his wife went on board this afternoon. I gave mrs Robbins Some Derections & relurnd.   at mr Robbins. wrote to Doct Barton.  
 

 

 September 6, 1794, Saturday

view full Diary page

 

Birth Doct Colmans Son.

Clear, a North wind. I was Calld to See the wife of Doct Colman who was in Labour at 1h 1/2 ys morn. Shee was Safe Deld of a fine Son at 11h 10m foren. I left her as well as Could be Expected & returnd home at 3h 30m aftern. I wrode to mr Robbins, find his Lady more Comfortable. I was at Capt Fillebrowns, all well.   at Doct Colmans. Birth 34th. at mr Robbinss & Capt Fillebrowns. Bot 4 oz Snuff @ 9d. 
 

 

 October 6, 1794, Monday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear. I have been to See mr Hodges Child, they think it is mending. Calld at Son Pollards, they are well, at mr Baxters, his wife is unwell, at Doct Colmans, all well. I had of him 1 oz Galbanum 1/3, Dito alloes 1/, _ oz myrr /7, one nutmeg 1/, 2 oz Clovs. mrs Colman made me a present of a thimble. I Calld at mr Hamlins, they are well. mr Ballard at Doct Coneys & mr Pollards. Cyrus bot m 3 pints Spirrits, Cost 1/20_.   at mr Hodges & others, at Doct Colmans.  
 

 

 October 10, 1794, Friday

view full Diary page

 

X. Birth David Sewalls Son. XX.

Cloudy. at mr Sewalls, they were intimidated & Calld Dr Page who gave my Patient [50] drops ot Laudenum which put her into Such a Stupor her pains (which were regular & promising) in a manner [Scant] till near night when Shee pukt & they returnd & Shee was Delivd at 7h Evn of a Son, her first Born. I left her Cleverly at 10 & walkt home. I receivd 12/ as a reward.   at Ditoes. Birth 39th. mr Perham Sleeps here. Dolly is unwell.  
 

 

 October 15, 1794, Wednesday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear. Selva moore here. I have wrote to Doct Barton, Bror Collens and Sister Walers, & went to See mrs Parker who is very unwell. Dolly gone to mr [Smiths].   at mr Parkers. wrote my friends at Oxford. Silva moore Sleeps [here].  
 

 

 October 22, 1794, Wednesday

view full Diary page

 

X. Birth Chandler Robbinss Son. XX.

Cloudy. mrs Robbins Linguerd till 4h pm when her illness Came on. Doct Parker was Called but Shee Did not wish to See him when he Came & he returnd home. Shee was Safe Deld of a Son, her first Born, at 10h 30m Evn & is as well as Can be Expected. I tarried & watcht. mrs Ney was there and Shee only, Except ye famely.   at mr Robbins. Birth 42nt. 
 

 

 February 14, 1795, Saturday

view full Diary page

 

X. Birth David Pollards 2nd Son. XX.

Clear. I was at home till Evn, 9 O Clock, when I was Calld to See David Pollards wife who was Dild of a fine Son at 10. I tarried all night. mr Stodard is very Sick at that house, Dilarious. Doct Page there to visit him, he was Calld in ye night.   at mr Pollards. Birth 9th. David Pollards wife is 27 years old this day. receivd 6/7 Novr 20 of Old Lady Pollard.  
 

 

 February 16, 1795, Monday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear. I tarried with my patient till after Dinner. left her and infant Cleverly. mr Stodard Seemd to have made feavour. Doct Page Bled him in ye feet this morn, he has been bled, Phissicked and Btistered before, in his Sickness. I Calld at mr Abbotts, Capins, and Son Pollards. Dagt Ballard is not so well as Could be wisht this aftern, her Sister Trask was there with a Lamb Son.   at Dittoes & other Neighbours.  
 

 

 April 29, 1795, Wednesday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear. I Came home, wrode a hors of Son Jonas, find mrs Young here helping Dolly Strike A harnis. Doct Cony here for a plan.   at Son Pollards.  
 

 

 May 10, 1795, Sunday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear & pleasant. my famely, Except my Self and Dolly, went to meeting at ye hook. mrss Holman, mr Trask & wife, Augusts Ballard, Jessy Bullin, Wm Parmer, Anna Bullin and Fanny Cocks Supt here. the Girls Sleep here, Ballard allso.   at home. receivd a Letter from Doct Barton of April 22nt, by mrss Holman.  
 

 

 May 19, 1795, Tuesday

view full Diary page

 

X. Birth James Blacks Daugt. XX.

Clear foren. I went to the hook, bot a Brass kettle wt 5 lb at 4/ pr lb of Capt FilleBrown. Cloudy aftern. I went to mr Pollards, was at mr Smiths, Pittss, Gills and Son Pollards. Came home at 5, Sett Down to write to Doct Barton and was Calld off by mr Hodges to See mrs Black. Shee was Deld at 7 of a Daugt, her 4th Child; number of Sex Equal. I tarried all night, my Patient was Exercisd with Some pain.   at ye hook & fort, at mr Blacks. Birth 22nt. raind at night. fee receivd June 4th.
