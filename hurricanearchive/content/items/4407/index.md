While wrought with grief, I had no idea what a blessing his passing was, nor what was in store for us the next five days. Sadly, as many other families with loved ones at Baptist, this did not happen. We didn\'t sleep much in the stifiling heat and nibbled on rations of graham crackers and peanut butter. Thursday morning we raced to the Tulane parking garage to watch the real horrors unfold. Hundreds of patients from Tulane and Charity hospitals were laying on the ground in the 90 degree heat, being fanned with cardboard pieces, and kept alive with hand held ventilators. They were unloaded of the backs of flat bead trucks by sweaty and exhausted hospital workers.
