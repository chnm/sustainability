Hospitals
Samuel Hodgdon
July 12th 1800
respecting the purchase
of sundries, for the use 
of the sick at Harpers
ferry Virga
[address written across length of sheet, perpendicular to above]
Israel Whelen Esquire
Purveyor
Philadelphia
Intendant of Stores Office
Philadelphia 12th July 1800
Sir,
The following hospital stores are immediately
for the use of the sick, under the care
of Doctor Charles Brown, at Harpers Ferry in
Virginia, vizt. fifteen gallons of madeira wine,
fifteen gallons of port wine, fifteen gallons of Brandy,
fifteen gallons of molasses, one hundred wt. of Coffee,
fifty wt. of Chocolate, tens of [illegible] tea, one
hundred pounds of muscovado sugar, one hundred
pounds of rice, twenty five pounds of barley, 
thirty gallons of vinegar, one pound of nutmegs
one pound of cloves, two pounds of cinnamon, ten
pounds of sage, twenty pounds of tamarinds, twenty
pounds of Raisins, and twenty pounds of prunes--
which I will thank you to purchase as soon as con
-venient, and have deposited in the public Store 
in this city.  I am, Sir, your most obt. serv.
Israel Whelen Esq.
Samuel Hodgdon
