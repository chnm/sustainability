NMAH Story: Story

I woke up that morning and there was a building on t.v. with fire and smoke spilling out the sides. Being almost fully asleep I had no idea what the building was. As I watched I saw it was the Twin Towers and that terrorists had run a plane into one of them, then I saw them jam a second plane into the remaining tower. My reaction was blunt because I was tired, I knew they did it for some kind of reason, but with sleep on my mind I would have to find out what the reason was later.

NMAH Story: Life Changed

My life hasn't changed really, only my hunger for the truth. The media makes it sound like America is 100 percent innocent. However, we were the ones that wanted to run a gas line through Afghanistan and not share any of the gas. And just like America, Afghanistan has terrorists too, they were bound to unleash their plans sooner or later, what better time than now?

NMAH Story: Remembered

I think we should remember that there is evil out in our world, in every country, and we should do what we can to stop it. The people need to know that by leaveing it up to the people in the N.Y.P.D the problems wont be fixed. E.g. officers in the N.Y.P.D are trying to get people like Alex Sanchez deported for belonging to an organization called Homies Unidos because they help to get drugs off the streets, kids out of gangs, and settle gang rivalries. Officers don't like this because without gangs and criminals they have no one to frame for their illegal doings, no one to harass, and nothing to do. So September 11th is a reminder that we need to stand up and take the power back!

NMAH Story: Flag

No I did not fly an American flag after September 11th. Both America and Afghanistan did wrong, to take sides is stupid. Someone might say, "Well what about all the innocent people that died on September 11th?" Well what about all the innocent people that were murderd by the Americans at war? So you see, both sides are wrong. As far as my feelings toward the American flag go, they haven't changed. It's still just a symbol for a country that is falling apart.
