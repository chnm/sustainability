Vietnam Resource List
Dept. of Mass Communications
Briar Cliff College
Ralph A. Swain, Asso. Prof.

Anti-war perspective:

Days of Decision: An Oral History of Conscientious Objectors in the
Military during the Vietnam War,  Gerald R. Gioglio. Broken Rifle Press:
Trenton, NJ, 1989.

I Refuse: Memories of a Vietnam War Objector, Donald L. Simons. Broken
Rifle Press: Trenton, N.J., 1992.

Long Time Passing, Myra MacPherson. Doubleday: New York, 1984.

The Sixties: Years of Hope, Days of Rage, Gitlin.       (A look at the
1960s free speech and anti-war movement)

Combat trauma:

Achilles In Vietnam: Combat Trauma and the Undoing of Character,
Jonathan Shay. Atheneum, 1994.

Farewell, Darkness: A Veteran's Triumph Over Combat Trauma, Ron Zaczek.
Naval Institute Press: Annapolis, MD, 1994.

Kerry: Agent Orange and an American Family, St. Martins Press, Cliff
Linedecker, Michael Ryan & Mureen Ryan, 1982.

Lives After Vietnam: The Personal Impact of Military Service.

Stress Disorders Among Vietnam Veterans, Charles R. Figley, Ph.D.,
Brunner/Mazel Inc., New York, 1978.

Vietnam: The Battle Comes Home, Nancy Howell-Koehler, ed. Morgan and
Morgan: Dobbs Ferry, NY, 1984.

The Wounded Generation: America after Vietnam, Sam Brown, Phillip
Caputo, James Fallows, Susan Jacoby, Tim O'Brein, Lucian K. Truscott,
James Webb, A Washington Press Book, Prentice Hall, Inc., Englewood
Cliffs, NJ, 1981.

Historical and Political:

A Bright Shining Lie: John Paul Vann and America in Vietnam, Neil
Sheehan. Random House, 1988.    (The political history of the debacle of
the Vietnam War from the early beginning of U.S. involvement with
military advisors)

Backfire: Vietnam-The Myths That Made U.S. Fight, etc., Loren Baritz.
Ballentine, 1985.

The Coast Guard at War: Vietnam, 1965-1975, Alex Larzelere. Naval
Institute Press: Annapolis, MD, 1997. (A detailed account of the
deployment of the U.S. Coast Guard in Vietnam for river, canal and
coastal interdiction)

End of the Line: The Siege of Khe Sanh, Terrence Maitland and Steven
Weiss, 1982.
(A detailed account of the siege that preceeded the TET 1968 attack in
Jan. 1968)

The Hidden History of the Vietnam War, John Prados.  Ivan R. Dee, inc.
19995.

Kiss the Boy's Goodbye: How the United States Betrayed its own POWs in
Vietnam, Monica Jensen-Stevenson. Penguin: NY, 1991.


Nam, Mark Baker. Quill Books, 1981.

Perfect War, The: The War We Couldn't Lose & How We Did, James W.
Gibson. Random House, 1988.

Raising the Stakes: The Vietnam Experience, Terrence Maitland and Steven
Weiss, 1982.

Remembering Heaven's Face, John Balaban, Simon & Schuster, 1992. (A
story of the personal experience of a consciencious objector working in
Vietnam for International Volunteer Services to complete his national
service requirement)

Secrecy & Democracy: The CIA in Transition, Stansfield Turner. Houghton
Mifflin: Boston, 1985.  (A history of the Central Intelligence Agency
with copious discussion of the CIA's role in Vietnam told by the former
director of the CIA)

Son Thang: An American War Crime, Gary D. Solis. Naval Institute Press:
Annapolis, MD, 1997.  (A detailed explanation of the murder of 16
non-combatant Vietnamese peasants by a U.S. Marine patrol in 1969)

The Ten Thousand Day War: Vietnam 1945-1975, Michael Maclear. St.
Martins Press, 1981.    (A detailed history of the Vietnam War beginning
with the first U.S. involvement.  This book rivals Karnow's Vietnam
epic)

Vietnam: A History, the First Complete Account of Vietnam at War,
Stanley Karnow.  Viking Press: New York, 1983.  (One of the most
complete accounts of the history of the Vietnam War.  This is a
companion to the PBS television series, Vietnam: A Television History)

Vietnam Order of Battle, Shelby Stanton. US News & World Report Books.

Winners and Losers, Gloria Emerson. Harvest Books: Harcourt, Brace &
Jovanovich, 1976.

Without Honor: Defeat in Vietnam & Cambodia, Arnold Isaacs. Johns
Hopkins University Press, 1983.

Novels (fiction):

Going After Cacciato,  Tim O'Brien. Houghton Mifflin, 1979.

In The Lake of the Woods, Tim O'Brien. Houghton Mifflin, 1995. (A
Vietnam veteran's return to the U.S. and dealing  with PTSD.  A murder
mystery)

Novel Without A Name, Duong Thu Huong. Morrow, 1995.  (set in 1974, this
story is about a North Vietnamese Army (NVA) captain who fought the
Americans and ARVN)

The Sorrow Of War, Bao Ninh.  Pantheon, 1995.  (this is about a young
NVA soldier
who is sent south to fight.  This book is a best-seller in Vietnam)

The 13th Valley, John Delvecchio. Bantam Books, 1982.

Oral history and Memoirs:

After TET, Ronald Spector. The Free Press (Macmillan): New York, 1993.

Bloods: An Oral History of Vietnam.     (The story of Vietnam told by
U.S. African-American soldiers)

Chickenhawk, Robert Mason. Viking Press, 1983. (A detailed account of
helicopter flight training, Vietnam combat and effects upon the pilot's
return home)

Dear America; Letters Home From Vietnam, Edelman Bernardo. Norton, 1985.
(A collection of letters written by U.S. soldiers to loved ones back
home)

Everything We Had; An Oral History of the Vietnam War, Al Santoli.
Ballantine, 1986.       (A combat veteran's experience in the Vietnam
War)

Fortunate Son; The Autobiography of Lewis B. Puller, Jr., Lewis Puller.
Bantom: New York, 1991.

Grunts, The, Charles Anderson. Presidio Press: San Rafael, 1976.

My Father, My Son, Elmo Zumwalt. (The story about U.S. Admiral Puller's
son who served in Vietnam and contracted cancer from exposure to Agent
Orange)

No More Vietnams, Richard Nixon.

Our War Was Different: Marine Combined Action Platoons in Vietnam, Al
Hemingway. Naval Institute Press: Annapolis, MD, 1994

Phantom Over Vietnam: fighter pilot, John Trotti. Presidio Press, 1984.
(The personal experiences of a U.S. fighter pilot during the Vietnam
War)

Rumor of War, Philip Caputo. Holt, Rinehart, Winston, 1977.     (The
personal story of a U.S. Army lieutenant in Vietnam)

We Were Soldiers Once...and Young: Ia Drang--The Battle That Changed the
War in Vietnam, Harold Moore and Joseph Galloway.       (A detailed
account from those who were in the battle of the Ia Drang Valley...the
first full-scale battle between North Vietnamese troops and the U.S.
Army First Air Cavalry Division)

Photography:

A Veteran's Album; A Photographic Memory Book of the Vietnam War, Jon
Schneider. Turner Pub. Co.: Paducah, KY, 1993.

The Vietnam War (Pictorial History), Edited by Ray Bonds, Crown
Publishers, 1979.

Tim Page's Nam, Tim Page.  Thames and Hudson, 1995      (The British
photo journalist's black and white, and color photos of the Vietnam War.
Page was wounded several times covering the war)

Vietnamese perspective:

In the Jaws of History, Bui Diem. HM, 1987.

In Their Defense: US Soldiers in the Vietnam War, Dr. Pham Kim Vinh.
Sphinx Pub. Inc.: Phoenix, AZ.  (A Vietnamese defense of the U.S. role
in the Vietnam War)

The Sorrow of War, A Novel of North Vietnam, Bao Ninh.  Pantheon Books,
NY. 1993.       (A personal account of the Vietnam War written by a
Vietnamese living in North Vietnam)

Novel Without A Name, Duong Thu Huong. William Morrow & Co., NY, 1995.
(The Vietnam War from the perspective of the "enemy.")

Vietcong Memoir, Trvong Nhu Tang. Harcourt Brace & W., 1985.    (The
Vietnam war from the side of the Viet Cong)

Vision Accomplished? The Enigma of Ho Chi Minh, N. Khac Huyen.
Macmillan, 1971.        (A Vietnamese criticism of Ho Chi Minh)





Women in combat:

A Piece of my Heart: The Stroies of Twenty-Six American Women who Served
in Vietnam, Keith Walker. Presidio Press: Novato, Ca, 199985.

In the Combat Zone: An Oral History of American Women in Vietnam,
Kathryn Marshall.  Little Brown: Boston, 1987.  (A collection of
personal accounts of the experiences of American women who served,
mostly as nurses, in Vietnam during the war.

Home Before Morning: The Story of an Army Nurse in Vietnam, Lynda Van
Devanter. Beaufort Books: New York, 1983.       (A classic account of
the experiences of an Army nurse in Vietnam and her troubles upon
returning home)

Cinema:

The Cinema of Oliver Stone, Norman Kagan.  Continuum Pub. Group, 1995.
(A study of this Vietnam veteran film director and his movies, including
several Vietnam films)

How The War Was Remembered: Hollywood and Vietnam, Albert Auster &
Leonard Quart.  Praeger: New York, 1988

Vietnam Veteran Films, Mark Walker, Scarecrow Press: Metuchen, NJ, 1991
(A compilation and description of films made by and about the Vietnam
War)


Swain's picks of feature films on Vietnam:

Although Hollywood cannot definitively produce the Vietnam movie that
depicts all the pathos, politics and individual experiences of the 3.5
million men and women who served in the Vietnam theater, the movies
below each represent in a small way what Professor Swain believes to be
fairly honest depictions of specific incidents, personal experiences and
overall mood of the Vietnam experience.

84 Charlie Mopic  (shot in cinema veritÃ© style, a military motion
picture cameraman
accompanies a squad on patrol)

Apocalypse Now  (Francis Ford Coppola's epic tale based upon the novel
Heart of
Darkness about an Army captain sent on a mission to terminate a U.S.
colonel)

Born On The Fourth of July  (Oliver Stone's story about a Vietnam vet
who comes
home a paraplegic)

Birdy   (the story about a Vietnam vet who suffers traumatic
psychological dysfunction)

Coming Home   (an early film about a disabled Vietnam vet)

The Deer Hunter  (Martin Scorcese's epic about steel mill buddies who
join the
Special Forces and go to Vietnam)

Don't Cry, It's Only Thunder   (a little known Japanese/American
production about
the experiences of a medical technician in worntorn Saigon)

Full Metal Jacket  (Stanley Kubrick's story about U.S. Marine training
and their
experiences in Vietnam during the Tet Offensive of 1968)

Good Morning Vietnam  (based upon the true story of Adrian Kronauer, an
Armed
Forces Vietnam Network radio DJ in Vietnam)


Indochine  (epic tale of the French in Vietnam shot in beautiful
locations)

The Killing Fields   (this is about the fall of Cambodia with the Khmer
Rouge
immediately after the fall of South Vietnam)

Platoon    (Oliver Stone's first Vietnam film and Academy Awards winner
about
the one-year Army tour in Vietnam)

Saigon    (an early, pre-U.S./Vietnam War film about the French
Indochina War)

The Ugly American   (an early film starring Marlon Brando about a
fictious
Asian country which, in reality, is based upon Vietnam before the
U.S.military buildup)
