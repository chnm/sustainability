<p>The Papers of the War Department project was featured in a <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2010/12/28/books/28transcribe.html?_r=1" title="NYTimes">recent article in the New York Times</a> about efforts to crowdsource documentary transcription. At the end of January 2011, PWD will implement the <a href="http://scripto.org">Scripto</a> transcription tool, allowing users to contribution transcriptions of the 55,000 documents in the PWD archive. With funding from the <a href="http://www.archives.gov/nhprc/" title="NHPRC">National Historical Publications and Records Commission</a> and the <a href="http://www.neh.gov/odh/" title="NEH ODH">Office of Digital Humanities at the National Endowment for the Humanities</a>, Scripto will provide editors of digital documentary collections with a way to harness the power of the public to build a base of transcription text that will improve document findability and ease of use.  Scripto is a PHP-based, free and open source plugin, that will connect to common content management systems.</p>
<p>If you are interested in participating in the project as a volunteer transcriber, please email <a href="mailto:sleon@gmu.edu">Sharon Leon</a>.</p>
