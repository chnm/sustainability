NMAH Story: Story

I was getting ready for school the morning of 9/11. I heard my grandmother crying to herself while watching the news on television. She had just heard that two planes had just crashed. I myself could not believe that our world trade centers were at this very moment being crashed into. Taking in the aftermath inside of me was terrible and scary. I read and read about 9/11, crying to myself, thinking how many lives were lost, how much damage Osauma Bin Laden and his crew had done. I read in People magazine about the calls that were made on flight 45, and realized how much courage those people had, even when they realized they were in danger. I had even heard the youngest kid on the flight was only 2. Now, I have a stronger feeling of - courage.

NMAH Story: Life Changed

Yes, it has changed me. I have a better outlook on the world, and i am able to open up and talk about how i have been feeling about 9/11

NMAH Story: Remembered

I think all of the firefighters, police, paramedics, and people who died should be remembered.
