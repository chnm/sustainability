November 26 1786
to Oliver Wolcott Junr. Esqr
Copied
Examined
New York November 28 1786
Dear Sir,
Your Letter of the 18th requesting an account stating of the 
accounts of the State of Connecticut is received & shall
be attended to — I am at a loss however, whether you 
have intended to requested all the accounts of the state extracted
from the different officers, or only those which have
arisen in mine.
Inclosed is an original Receipt given by Jona. Philips and John Thomas for 10,283 76/90 Dols in Certificates,
which they have not accounted for, I take
the liberty therefore to request that you will call
on them to deliver into you the vouchers that they
have taken for these certificates, and where they 
have not been issued, the certificates themselves,
and in case they refu this is refused, that you would
cause the obligation to be put into suit — a certificate]
from your office sufficient to which will authorize a charge
against the state will be a sufficient voucher
for these Gentlemen.
I am, with great respect, Dr Sir
Your Ob Servt.
[to] Oliver Wolcott Jur Esqr
