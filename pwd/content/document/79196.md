Geo. Harrison
Dated
Dec.r 1st1800
277
Philad.a December 1 th 1800
Sir
We have made enquiery for Simon Messinger, for the
purchase of knowing from hom the particulars respecting the Machine
directed, by the late Sect.ry of War, to be made for the use of the
Navy, as yet we have not been able to find him, We also wrote 
M.r McHenry on the Subject, we expect his answer herewith, and
his direction, on the back of Messingers Letter to him in your
possessoion, for Mr Francis to advance the $ 100 with the Sufficient
Vouchers to Obtain via Credit for that Sum ~ It is possible
the Instrument has not, nor never will be comblated in which
can we showed Suppon the Representatives of the late Agent
and not to loon the advances made by the express direction of
the Sectry of War, whose power at that time embraced the
affairs of the Navy -- If we can find Messinger we will obtain
every information in our power respecting the Instrument.
I am Sir
wih much Esteem
Your very hble Serv.nt
Geo Harrison [undecipherable]
Thomas Turner Esq
