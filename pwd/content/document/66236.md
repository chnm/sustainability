description: 'In return for Indian friendship, General Wilkinson has promised an annual stipend to a Chickasaw chief, Wolf''s Friend, who is now in Philadelphia.  Considers different points on the wisdom
  of filling such a request, including: that Wolf''s Friend comes from a faction of Chickasaw not yet closely allied to the U.S.; no individual chief has ever been given a stipend, only part of the sum
  for the nation; foreign relations with Spain, etc.  Recommends giving an equal gift to each of the heads of the Chickasaw factions, in return for support of the U.S.  Also informs of a sum promised to
  a Cherokee chief for his help in concluding a U.S.-Cherokee treaty.  Notes that Wolf''s Friend desires an audience with the president.  Mentions that a Seneca chief is in the city looking to collect on
  gifts promised to that nation; discusses how sums for this purpose may be drawn from the Bank of the United States.'
