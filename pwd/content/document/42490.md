- pray you to use me in any respect you think
- end to the indian disturbances
- possession of the posts
- endeavor to find out the number of indians in the actions
- bears the women & shildren
- necessity of making peace
- depend on you for their trade
- cannot now live without cloathing and ammunition
- bows and arrows & skin coats are out of fashion with them
- blanket & the gun
- complete command over all of the indians
- numerous tribes are within our limits
- assemble in great numbers
- families in the mean are left to suffer
- merchandise used in the fur trade
- established the bounds of the United States
- fate of the action
- distance from that place to where the battle was fought being about two hundred & fifty miles
- effect the surrender
- held by the British
- indian affairs will be managed with more ease and certainty
- no treaty should be made with Great Britain
- afford me great pleasure to render you service in this or any other way
- intelligence that may come to me shall be immediately communicated to you
- detail to you any information he may have
- refer you to himself
- true account of all that can have come to his knowledge
- Cayuga Indian
- brought of all the books of papers and carried them to Quebec
- rec'd your favor
