description: "Cited in Knox to Wayne, 10/26/1792.\r\n\r\nWayne is gratified to learn that Governor Blount was merely alarmed by the report of war and not more seriously affected.  He worries about the safe\
  \ return of Chief Cornplanter; according to the deposition of William May and intelligence gathered from the Indians, there is much evidence that some \x93premeditated mischief\x94 is intended to be carried\
  \ out against Cornplanter.  \r\n\r\nWayne provides dispatches that he recently received, one of which provides a circumstantial account of General Wilkinson\x92s second tour to the site of General St.\
  \ Clair\x92s defeat.  He informs Knox that Major Rudolph headed down the Kenhawa River with needed clothing, arms, and accoutrements for the soldiers at the mouth of the river.  Wayne\x92s time of late\
  \ has been spent preparing for the move to winter quarters.\r\n\r\nWayne remarks on the latter part of a letter from the President discussing the redistribution of the Sublegions, and Wayne\x92s notion\
  \ that neither possesses a \x93solid skeleton.\x94  There are not more than 1100 men, excluding those who are sick, and the actual number fit for duty is 824 men.  Wayne feels it would be very difficult\
  \ to find a Sublegion for Marietta without raising more effectives for the Legion, and so this needs to be a priority.\r\n\r\nWayne also encloses an extract from a letter written by Mr. Belle which reveals\
  \ he is too optimistic about the price and feasibility of procuring forage in Kentucky and transporting it to Fort Washington."
