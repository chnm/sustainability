New York 1 April 1786
Dear Sir

Having a leisure moment I have settled the accts. of Cap. Alexr Ramsay, and enclosed you will receive
a Certificate No. 93,894, for sixty eight 16/90 Dollars, the amount of said account — I have also enclosed
you the accounts of Cap. Ross for rations which I am
unable to liquidate, as no rations are now admitted
previous to the 1 June 1778: This was given to Mr Pierce
in his instructions from the Superintendant of
Finance, as all the Officers previous to that period were
allowed to draw all the rations due them, and this
Office having no checks on those accounts they
cannot be admitted — 
I cannot give you any information of James
Willson until I know the regiment he was in
previous to his death — You will please to
send me a receipt for Mr. Ramsay's Certificate. 
I am, Sir, 
with Esteem 
Your Hble Servt  
Jos. Howell Junr. 
John Nicholson Esquire
[Stamp] Received from the Auditor for the Interior Department, May 7 1895.  R.P.O 415348.
