 February 14, 1795, Saturday

view full Diary page

 

X. Birth David Pollards 2nd Son. XX.

Clear. I was at home till Evn, 9 O Clock, when I was Calld to See David Pollards wife who was Dild of a fine Son at 10. I tarried all night. mr Stodard is very Sick at that house, Dilarious. Doct Page there to visit him, he was Calld in ye night.   at mr Pollards. Birth 9th. David Pollards wife is 27 years old this day. receivd 6/7 Novr 20 of Old Lady Pollard.  
 

 

 February 15, 1795, Sunday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear. I tarried with my Patient part of the Day & all night. Calld to See mrs Baxter, Shee is recovering. mrs Voce & I wint to mr Childs at Evng, all well there. mr Stodard a little more Composd.   at Dittoes.  
 

 

 February 16, 1795, Monday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear. I tarried with my patient till after Dinner. left her and infant Cleverly. mr Stodard Seemd to have made feavour. Doct Page Bled him in ye feet this morn, he has been bled, Phissicked and Btistered before, in his Sickness. I Calld at mr Abbotts, Capins, and Son Pollards. Dagt Ballard is not so well as Could be wisht this aftern, her Sister Trask was there with a Lamb Son.   at Dittoes & other Neighbours.  
 

 

 February 20, 1795, Friday

view full Diary page

 

 Cloudy and a little Snow. this Day is the annevercyra of my Birth. I have Seen 60 years roall away and but few more must look to See. God grant I may for Death prepared bee!!! I have been at home, finisht a pair of hoes for mr Ballard.   at home.  
 

 

 February 28, 1795, Saturday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear and very Pleasant. Sally washt house & Scourd puter, Dolly asisted Some. I have been at home. Son Jon a here.   at home.  
 

 

 March 2, 1795, Monday

view full Diary page

 

X. Birth mr Childss Dagt. XX.

Clear & pleast. mr Gill & wife Sett out for Winslow. I was Calld at 11m to See mrs Child. Shee was Deld at three pm of a Dagt. Benn Porter Came there at Evn for me to go and See his wife. I find Dolly very ill with the mumps when I Came home. Jany 29 = I recd 2 Crowns.   at mr Childs. Birth 10th. Dolly is very ill. Benn Porter Sleeps here. mr Ballard is gone to Sidney.  
 

 

 March 4, 1795, Wednesday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear & Pleast. I was at I was at [sic] Ditoes. Shee is Cleverly, and her famely.   at Ditoes. Son Jona 32 years old ys day.  
 

 

 March 8, 1795, Sunday

view full Diary page

 

 A Severe Storm, the wind S E. mrs Porter was ill, had women Calld.   at Ditoes.  
 

 

 March 9, 1795, Monday

view full Diary page

 

Birth Benn Porters Son.

Clear. mrs Porter in Labour all Day and was Deliverd of a Son at 8 hours Evn. her Case was very Difucult and Shee was very faint after Delivery till about midnight, when Shee revivd and got Some rest. mrs Carr watcht.   at Porters. Birth 11th. A Ship [wreck] mentioned in this Days [herald].  
 

 

 March 12, 1795, Thursday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear and pleasant. I went to mr Craigs, Shee made me a present of half yd Cambric & Do of CLaoth. mrs Burtun & Thwing there. I Calld to See mrs Child, Shee and infant are as well as Can be expected; at mr Pollards, Davids wife is about again. I Calld at Son Pollards & Jonas, all well.   at mr Craigs and other Neigrs. mr Ballard went to mr John Jones. Ephm gone for hay.  
 

 

 March 15, 1795, Sunday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear. mr Ballard attended Public worship at the hook. mr Bailey Calld here, took Tea and waited on mrs Church home. mr Capin and wife, mr Gill & Lambart & D. Livermr here at Evng. I have been at home. Polly Town here.   at home. mrs Church went home. Polly Town Came here.  
 

 

 March 17, 1795, Tuesday

view full Diary page

 

Birth Savage [Boltons] Son.

Clear and pleasant. Dolly is quillting a Coat for her Self. I Combd flax foren, was Calld to See mr Partrages Child who is very unwell, has a Cough. I was Calld from there to See Savage Boltons wife who was in Labour and was Deld at 6h pm of a Son. I tarried there all night, my Patient Cleverly.   at mr Partridges & Savage Boltons. Birth 12th. receid 1_ Bushl of apples in the fall, not very good.  
 

 

 March 18, 1795, Wednesday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear and very Pleasant. I Came home from mr Boltons, left my patients Cleverly. Calld at Son Pollards, all well there. my Dagt and mrs Gill Came here. mr Ballard has been to Winthrop.   at [Savage] [Boltons].

 A S Easter, Cleard off aftern. Son Pollard Came and Conducted his wife home. I was Calld to See a mrss Gordin who resides at mr N. Shaws, tarried there all night.   at [mr] [Gordins].

 March 21, 1795, Saturday

X. Birth Capt James Springers Dagt. XX.

Clear and pleast. mrs Springer was Deld at 7h morn of a Dagt. I left her and infant Cleverly and returnd to mr Gordins, find her about. from there I returnd home, find my famely all well. mr Ballard is gone from home. Sally Cocks is gone to her Dadys. Shee went yesterdy.   at [Capt] [Springers] and [Gordins]. Birth 13[th].  
 

 

 March 26, 1795, Thursday

 Clear. I was Calld at 11h last Evn to mr Gordins, his wife unwell. mrs Black Sent for me, I Could not go.    
 

 

 March 27, 1795, Friday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear and Cool. I was at mr Gordins, his wife was Deld (at 4h 30m morn) of a Dagt. I left her & inft as well as Could be Expected and arivd at home at 11h foren. I receivd [3] lb Cottne @ 2/4 pr lb. I Dind and went to See mrs Black, find her better yn my fears. I Calld to See mrs Smith and mrs Capin and at Son Pollards.   at Ditoes. Birth 14th. Calld to Thoms Kenneys at 11h Evng.

 Clear. I was at mr Kinneys, his wife very unwell all Day. Seems more Comfortable at Evn. part of her women went home.   at Ditoes. my [patient] very ill.

 Snowd. I was at mr Kinneys, his wife is better, her women all went home. I tarried this night.   at Ditoes.

 Clear and warm. I was at Ditoes, am informd that John O. Pages wife was Deld this morn and her Child is Dead.   at Ditoes.

 April 4, 1795, Saturday

view full Diary page

 

[Birth Lathrops Dagt].

Cloudy and Clear by turns. mrs Latp was able to work till near SunSet when Shee was more unwell. Shee was Safe Deld of a Dagt at 8h Evn and is Comfortable. this is her 5th Child, and one of them is Desceast . I was informd at Evn that a mr Watson was Drouned at Joness mill this aftern.   at Ditoes. Birth 16th. mr Watson was Drownd at Jonsis.  
 

 

 April 5, 1795, Sunday

view full Diary page

 

 rainy morn, thunder with it. I left my Patients Cleverly and Came home at 10h morn, find my famely well. mrs Gill and all our men attended worship at ye hook. mr Ballard, my Self and Sons and S. G. Moore attended Funeral of a mr Watson who was Drownd yesterday. the prayer was made by ye revd mr Gillit. the remains were lodgd in the meeting house. John O. Pages infant was interd this day.   at Ditoes and at the funeral of mr Watson. P. & A. Livermores here at [Evn]  
 

 

 April 13, 1795, Monday

view full Diary page

 

X. Birth mr Capins Dagt. XX.

Cloudy the most of the Day. I was Calld by mr Capin at 5h morn to See his wife who was in Labour, and was Safe Delivd of a Dagt at 1 pm, and I left her and infant Cleverly and returnd at 3. I was Calld to Capt Molloys.   at mr Capins. Birth 17th. Snowd at night.
