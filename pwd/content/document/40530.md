- had no reputation nor did I wish that the established rules of rank
- should be infringed to benefit John
- all I aimed at was to secure to him that rade which he would obtain by a personal application in virtue of his former commission
- if a company would in that way fall to his lot
- must be content with a lower grade
- requestsme to apply for as commission for him
- new corps was to be raised
- eligible a station
- sentiments you express as to the future propriety
- appears too much reason
- toward the government of the Union
- great alteration in the habits & feelings of the people has taken the place for the better
- respectability of government
- give me a strong impression of the necessity of its being supported
- habits of industry & frugality are taking plan this of
- general commercial arrangements
- private happiness be more generally enjoyed
- will daily extend its protection and blessings
- public mind to judge from this part of the Union has kept pace with the timing
- wonderful success & faculty
- Secretary's report should be adopted
- joint support of all classes of public creditors
- present ideas
- growing idea that the manners contracted during the war must be done away
- every class of citizens must expect only to thrive by the means commonly successful in a time of peace
- sentiments impressed on the minds of the body of the people
- advantages they will derive soon from the system proposed by the secretary of the Treasury
- received your obliging letter by the last post
- thank you for your friendly itentions both as to me & my son John
