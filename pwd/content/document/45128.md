description: Writing from Savannah, Seagrove acknowledges receipt of talks from Mr. Townsend and expresses satisfaction with the peaceful tone. Plans to meet at Oakmulgee 10 September 1793. Notes that it
  was General Washington who  ordered the Georgia militia back from the bad towns in order to give the Creeks time to punish those who committed robbery and murder at Traders Hill St. Marys. Comments on
  the unfortunate death of David Cornell. Plans to settle misunderstanding over horses. Asks that White Lieutenant take possession of Alexander McGillivray's effects that belonged to the United States.
