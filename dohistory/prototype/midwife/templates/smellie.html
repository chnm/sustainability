The handles and the lower part of the Blades may as here be covered with any durable Leather, but the Blades ought to be wrapped round with something of a thinner kind, which may be easily renewed when there is the least suspicion of vernereal Infection in a former Case; by being thus covered, the Forceps have a better hold, and mark less the Head of the Child. For their easier Introduction, the Blades ought likewise to be greased with Hog'-lard... The Forceps were at first contrived to have the Foetus, and prevent, as much as possible, the use of sharp Instruments; but even to this salutary method recourse ought not to be had but Cases where the degree of force requires to extract will not endanger by its consequences the life of the Mother. For, by the imprudent use of the Forceps, much more harm may be done than good. (from Smellie, Abridgement of the Practice of Midwifery.)
