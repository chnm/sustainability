Found: some pictures of forceps that Dr. Page might have used in a delivery.


One can learn about the past not only from written documents, but also from images and artifacts.

Forceps came into greater use during Martha Ballard's and Dr. Page's lifetimes. Doctors whose training came primarily from medical schools and medical books used them; midwives, generally, did not. How might you uncover the reasons that account for this difference in their practices?

Notice that the written captions accompanying the images of forceps recommend wrapping the blades in leather and changing the leather if there is any sign of venereal infection. When instruments, such as forceps, were introduced into childbirth, the rates of infection increased because the instruments used were not clean. Decomposed matter left on instruments (and hands) caused puerperal fever to spread reaching epidemic proportions in some places and times. It was not until the 1840s that Dr. Oliver Wendell Holmes hypothesized and Dr. Ignaz Semmelweis later proved that the cause of the fever was discovered to be a result of poor sanitary practices.

Unfortunately, some physicians thought this hypothesis couldn't be true because it did not mesh with the commonplace conception of doctors: Doctors were supposed to help patients, not contribute to their demise. One incredulous doctor remarked that he was a gentleman and that gentlemen's hands were clean!

Can you think of analogous conflicts in contemporary life?
