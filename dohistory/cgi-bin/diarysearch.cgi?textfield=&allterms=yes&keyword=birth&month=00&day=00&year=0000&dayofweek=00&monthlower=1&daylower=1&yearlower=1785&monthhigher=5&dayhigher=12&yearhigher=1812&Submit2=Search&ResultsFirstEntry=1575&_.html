 August 29, 1797, Tuesday

view full Diary page

 

X. Birth Capt Gershom Coxs 2nd Dagt. XX.

Clear and pleast. I was Calld at 5h 30m morn to See the wife of Capt Gershom Cox who is in Labour. the [foets] was in an unnatural posetion but I Brot it into a proper Direction and Shee was Safe Deld at 7h morn of a fine Dagt, her Second Child, both of the Same Sex. we took Breakfast at 9 and I was Coming home but the inft appeard to be not So well as I Could wish to See it, and I tarried using means for its relief till after nn when I left it Seemingly better but not without Concern. I left Old Lady Cox with them.   at Capt Gershom Coxss. Birth 43d. receivd 9/8_ as a reward. mr Ballard and Hoit at the Meddow.  
 

 

 September 5, 1797, Tuesday

view full Diary page

 

Birth Andrew Goodins first X Dagt by his prest wife XX.

Clear Except Some ligt Showers. I was Calld at 3h morn to go to See the wife of Andrew Goodin who is in Labour. I arivd there at 4. Shee was Safe Deld at 12 & 40m of a fine Dagt. I left her and inft Cleverly at 4h pm. mr Goodin bestowd 12/ as a reward. I find mr Ballard is gone to Dresden. Hepsy has been washing. EbenZ Haild Esqr Sleeps here ys night.   at mr Andw Goodins. Birth 44th. recd 12/ as a reward.  
 

 

 September 7, 1797, Thursday

view full Diary page

 

 Cloudy and Some rain aftern. mr Ballard Sett out for to Survey for mr Reubin Fairfield. I went to the hook as far as Capt Neys. I receivd 6/ of his Lady in part for assisting her march 9th, 1796. I Calld at a number of the Neighrs.   at Capt Neys and other Neighbours.  
 

 

 September 9, 1797, Saturday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear. I was at home foren Sewing, was Calld aftern to See mrs Whilwels infant who is unwell with a Cold. I applyd a plaster to the Stomach and Onions to the feet and gave it a Syrrip of mullin. it Soon revivd and I returnd home. Calld at Son Lambarts, they are well. the midle Pier of the Kenebec Bridg was finisht and there were 7 Discharges of the field piece and 3 Cheirs.   at mr Whilwels, his inft unwell. mrss Cyfers & Savage been here.  
 

 

 September 10, 1797, Sunday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear and very pleast. mr Ballard is not yet returnd. Cyrus & Hoit attended at the fort, Hepsy at the hook. Thankfull Calld here. I Did not feel able to walk to meeting and tarried at home.   at home.  
 

 

 September 17, 1797, Sunday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear. I mended mr Ballards Coat foren, went to meeting aftern. I Saw a Girl take a fan out of mr James Pages Seat and put it in her pocket. I informd mr Page who went to the Seat Shee was in and Shee Deliverd it up after denying Shee had Seen it. I Did not know her name but am informd it is Ellis.   at meeting. Revd mr Stone informd us he proposes a visit to his friends.  
 

 

 September 21, 1797, Thursday

view full Diary page

 

Birth mrs Getchels Son.

Clear and pleast. I was Calld at 4hm to See a mrss Getchel at the hook who was in Labour with her first Child, her husband at Sea. Shee was Deld at 11h of a fine Son. I tarried with her till till [sic] 6 pm when I left her and inft Cleverly and returnd home.   at [mrs] Gitchels. Birth 45th. march [11], 1798 receivd 6/ in part.  
 

 

 September 28, 1797, Thursday

view full Diary page

 

X. Birth mr Gows Son. XX.

Clear and very pleast. I was Calld at 1h 40m in morn to See mrss Gow. Shee was Safe Deld at 4h 40m of a fine Son, her 3d Child. I left them Cleverly and returnd at 11h. mrs Moore Calld here. I went to see mrs Dingly.   at mr Gows. Birth 46th. recd 9/ as a reward.  
 

 

 September 29, 1797, Friday

view full Diary page

 

X. Birth [Wm Stratens] Dagt. XX.

Clear and very pleast. I was takeing Care of apples which were brot from the Bolton farm foren, was Calld at 1h 35m pm to See the wife of mr Wm Straton of Hallowell who was in Labour, and was Safe Deld at 2h 15m of a Dagt, her Second Child. I returnd at 7h Evng. I receivd 12/ as a reward.   at mr Stratens. Birth 47th. recd 12/ as a reward. Cyrus Brot 12 Bushels apples home from ye boltn place.  
 

 

 October 1, 1797, Sunday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear and very pleast. mr Ballard & Hoit went to meeting at ye hook and attended at ye interment of Joshua Learned who has lately resided at the farm of mr Charls Vahun at hallowell. Son Lambart & Richd Foster with their wives, and Son Ephm, Aguslus Ballard, Thankfull Godfray Dind with us, we had a Boild Dish and roast Chickens.   at home. the remains of Joshua Learned, who Expird 29 ult, were interd. I was Calld at 11 O Clock to See mrss Densmore.  
 

 

 October 2, 1797, Monday

view full Diary page

 

X. Birth mr Thos Dinsmores Dagt. XX.

Cloudy morn & rain. mrss Densmore was Safe Deld at 4h 30m Morn of her 9th Dagt and twelvth Child, and is Cleverly. I larried with her till the 11 [haur] lefet her and inft as well as Could be Expected. I receivd 6/ as a reward for my Service. mr Ballard is gone to Son Jonas, Cyrus to Pittstn, Hoit Carting Bark for feuell.   at mr Densmores, 9th Dagt & 12 Child. recd 6/ Cash. Birth 48th for ys year, I allso recd a Beef heart.  
 

 

 October 6, 1797, Friday

view full Diary page

 

Birth Isaac Savages ye 3d, Dagt.

Clear morn. I was Calld at 3h m to See the wife of Isaac Savage ye 3d, Shee was in Labour, and was Deld at 4 of a Dagt, her Second Child. I returnd home at 6h 30m. mr Ballard & Hoit went to Jonas. Old Lady Coutch Came here and dind, then went on for home.   at Isaac Savages. Birth 49th. 
 

 

 October 11, 1797, Wednesday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear. I have been at home. Old Lady Coutch Calld here, dind with me.   at home. Cyrus at Pittstn. 
 

 

 October 22, 1797, Sunday

view full Diary page

 

 A Clear Day, raind at Evng. we went to meeting but mr Stone was not returnd. I Calld at Son Pollards, tarried there a little while then Shee Came with me to Son Lambarts, where we find mr Gill and wife and mrss Kitridg Came in. we all partook of a fine Pigg and other good things. I Calld to See mrss Densmore as I Came home, find her as well as Could be Expected.   at Sons Pollards & Lambarts, Allin is 15 months old ys day.  
 

 

 October 25, 1797, Wednesday

view full Diary page

 

Birth Wilm Whites Son.

rainy and Snow morn. I was Calld about 6 hours to Moses Parmers to See his wife at 7, from there to Wm Whites. Calld back to Parmers at 3h pm. mrss White Calld mrss Hinkley and was Deld at 4 of a Son.   at Moses Parmers and Wilm Whites. Snowd this Morn.  
 

 

 October 26, 1797, Thursday

view full Diary page

 

X. Birth Moses Parmers Son. XX.

Clear and pleast. mrss Parmer was Deld of her first Child, a Son, between the hours of 12 and 1 morn. Shee has been very ill the first half of the night, but is Cleverly after being put to bed. I went from there to mr Whites, find his wife Safe Deld of a Son and Cleverly. I walkt from there Capt Coxes to Whites and back to mrs Husseys, then Crost the river with mr Bullin and walkt from mr Shepherds to Parmers, then wrode a Colt home on which woman never was before. I went to mr Dingleys after I Came home, mr Ballard allso. he returnd from his Journey this aftern, in health and Safety.   at Parmers, Whiles. Capt Cox is at Charlss and Gershoms, mr Bullins, Husseys and Dingleys. Birth 50. mr Ballard returnd, receivd 9/ of mrs Parmer June 28th, 1798.  
 

 

 October 31, 1797, Tuesday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear. mr Ballard went to Jonas, Cyrus to mr Moores to tend mill. Hepsy washt. we got in 70 Cabages, Churnd and made a Chees. Revd mr Gillit Calld here. mr Allin, the Cloather Calld and I paid him what was Due for Dresing our Cloath. Ezra Allin left acount of taxes.   at home, it is now 11h Evng.  
 

 

 November 3, 1797, Friday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear and warm. Son Jona brot his Dagt here. mr Ballard and I attended Lecture, there were but a small number there. I Calld at Son Pollards, find little Hannah in distress by Costivness. we used means which relievd her. we Calld at Son Lambarts, find them all about. my Dagt Seems not well. I removd my Rhue and Camomile from where the were in to the Gardin. little Jack Ballard Said if it Should die you will know better next time.   at Lecture & Calld to See my Childn. mr Dingley returnd from Boston.  
 

 

 November 6, 1797, Monday

view full Diary page

 

 very rainy morn. I was Calld at 25m after midnight to See the Lady of mr James Child who was very unwell thro ye day. I tarried with her thro this night. Shee Seemd more Comfortable at 10h Evn.   at mr Childs, his wife is very unwell.  
 

 

 November 7, 1797, Tuesday

 Cloudy morn, Snowd at 10h morn. I was at mr Hamlins Store, left mrss Child near night. find the roads very bad. Calld at Son Lambards, tarried there all night.   at ditoes; at mr Hamlins and Son Lambarts.  
 

 

 November 10, 1797, Friday

view full Diary page

 

 rainy morn. I was Calld to See my Dagt, Shee was very unwell, but got Some better. I went to See Dagt Pollard.   at Ditoes.  
 

 

 November 11, 1797, Saturday

view full Diary page

 

X. Birth Son Lambart Dagtr. XX.

rainy, it was this morn, not yesterday. I went to mr Lambarts, Shee was ill all day, her women were Calld in the Evng and Shee Safe Deld at 8h 45m of a fine Dagt. her Second Child. Dagt Pollard tarried all night.   at Son Lambarts. Birth 51st. recd 12/.  
 

 

 November 12, 1797, Sunday

view full Diary page

 

X. Birth James Childs Son. XX.

rainy and froze. I was Calld from Son Lambards at 3h ys morn to See mrss Child who was in Labour. Shee was Safe Delivird at 6h 45m of a fine Son, her fifth Child. I left her Cleverly at 10 and returnd to Son Lambards, tarrie with them this night.   at Ditoes & mr Childs. Birth 52nt. Decr 1802 recd 10/6 of mr Child for performing ys [   ].  
 

 

 November 14, 1797, Tuesday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear and very pleast. I have been at home till Evng, was then Calld to See mrss Child who had an ill turn. I took Care of her thro the night.   at mr Childs.  
 

 

 November 20, 1797, Monday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear and Cold. mr Ballard went to Son Jon as. I have Spent the day Nursing Allin Lambard, he has oftne Calld for his Pah and Mah, but has not Cried very much.   at home. Allin Lambard here.
