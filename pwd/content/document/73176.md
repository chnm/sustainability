description: Hamilton believes that the Articles of War need to be completely overhauled, which would take some time, and suggests improvements for the interim. There should be greater clarity relating
  to the power to appoint a general courts martial, specifically giving the President discretionary authority to empower officers other than those designated by the Articles of War to declare a general
  courts martial. The Commanding General of an army should have the power to decide on sentences. Hamilton addresses possible punishments for desertion, including confinement and labor.
