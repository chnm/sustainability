description: Notes that the military situation on the whole seems favorable, especially in terms of avoiding war with any European powers.  Is distressed by reports of differences between Generals Wayne
  and Wilkinson; assures Wilkinson that the only mention of him in letters received from Wayne have praised him.  Knox and the president are both in favor of a conciliation.  Discusses dissension in the
  army; dismisses Wilkinson's inquiry about Major General Scott being placed in command of the Legion of the United States.  Assures Wilkinson that his military reputation is intact, and informs him that
  he is soon retiring as Secretary of War.
