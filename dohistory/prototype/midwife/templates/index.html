Weaving Histories Electronic Sample
part of the Film Study Center's application to the NEH Public Programs Division



About this sample:

As an "electronic sample," we have built a prototype for a small portion of the interactive game that lies at the heart of the proposed Web site. This sample corresponds to the Doing History pathway in Area E: Dr. Page and Parthenia's autopsy (please refer to Diagram B in the written proposal). (Parthenia's autopsy is covered in segments not implemented for this sample.)

As you look at the sample, please keep in mind the following:

  • target viewing capabilities
    This sample has been designed to be seen on monitors with at least 16-bit color, and a fairly high speed of connection (ISDN or above). On the final Web site users will see graphics and pages appropriate to their hardware and connection speed (information will be obtained from the user about his/her viewing capabilities which will be used to customize his/her experience of the site; see Appendix E: Technical Plan in the written proposal for more details.)

  • browser compatibility
    This sample has been tested in Netscape, version 3.0 and above, and Internet Explorer, version 3.0 and above. The final Web site will be designed for, and tested on, the widest possible range of browsers and platforms. If you choose to view this sample with Internet Explorer, you will need to make sure you have a Quicktime plug-in installed (available from http://www.apple.com/quicktime/sw/). The final website will not compel people to install plug-ins. Note: Navigator 4.03 for the Macintosh is known to have a bug which interferes with proper display of this sample site.

  • video/audio delivery
    On the final Web site, the download time for video and audio files will be greatly reduced by the use of streaming technologies. Users who get the auxillary CD-ROM will have even faster access to those large media files.

  • auxillary CD-ROM
    The auxillary CD-ROM(s) will contain the highest resolution and highest quality media files used on the Web site. Those who choose to purchase the CD-ROM (at a nominal price) will enjoy greatly reduced download times and highest quality media. Those without the CD-ROM will be able to download the high resolution files from the web site, or see lower resolution versions instead.

  • ongoing revisions
    Over the next few weeks before the panel meeting we will be making a number of improvements to this sample Web site. So, if you are looking at this in mid-March, please check back at the beginning of April.

  • original documents
    We have had to use readily available copies of original historical documents--most visuals were scanned from xeroxes or amateur photographs. For the final Web site, with adequate resources, we will scan from high-quality originals to create the highest-quality images. Also, full-screen versions of original documents will be made available on the final Web site.

  • in this game prototype, supplementary questions are posed rhetorically alongside primary documents, and hints are available for the asking. In the final game, players will earn points by answering the supplemental questions correctly. They will then be able to exchange points for hints as they play the game. Where appropriate, the hints will come from Laurel Ulrich and the filmmakers in the form of anecdotes.


    Please let us know if you experience any difficulties with this sample. Due to limited resources at this time, we have not been able to do as much testing as we would have liked; it is likely that some bugs remain. If you email us (fsc@fas.harvard.edu), we will make every attempt to correct the problem in time for the panel meeting.

    If you have not yet read the the written proposal, you may want to read about Martha Ballard and her diary before beginning the sample.
