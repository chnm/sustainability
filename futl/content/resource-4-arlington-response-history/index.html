Resistance to Integration Elsewhere in Virginia

Schools in Norfolk, Charlottesville, and Warren County desegregated in February 1959 alongside Arlington. Many other schools, though, had to wait much longer.

In Prince Edward County, the school district where Barbara Johns led a student strike 8 years earlier to protest unequal schools (a case that became part of the original Brown v Board decision) schools were closed in 1959 rather than obey a court order to integrate. They remained closed for 5 years.

White students in the county attended the Prince Edward Academy supported by county and state funds while African American students had no public schools to attend. These schools, known as “segregation academies,” were established in many Virginia districts to avoid desegregation.

In 1964, ten years after Brown v Board, the U.S. Supreme Court declared these academies unconstitutional and Prince Edward schools reopened.
