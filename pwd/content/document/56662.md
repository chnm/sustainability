63
					Pittsburg 24.February 1797
Sir
	Your letter of the 17th instant is come to hand accompanied by the 
news papers.  The Packet for Col. Sargent shall go forward this 
Evening by Ensign Smith to Fort Washington in whose charge
I am sending the Chickasaw Indian's Goods addressed to the [ QÂ ?]
Master as you formerly [illegible] , but I have some reason to apprehend
that some neglect may take place with respect to these goods
being sent to Nashville.  I therefore wish you would mention
that business to the Secretary of War.  You will find enclosed 
Cap. E. D. Turner's draft on Wm Simmons at sight in
my favor for 80 dollars which will you will be pleased to receive
and place to my credit.
	I have this day embraced an opportunity of obtaining
by draft 1850 dollars, viz. by draft on you in favor of Geo.
Stevenson at 15 days sight for 1000 also in favor of Stephen
Leager at 15 days for 530 dollars & one in favor of Wm
Christy at 8 days sight for 300 dollars.  These drafts I hope
you will be en abled to pay before they come due
		I am
		Sir
		Your mo. ob. st.  I.C.
Samuel Hodgdon esq
	Philadelphia
