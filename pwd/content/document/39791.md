New York 6, March 1789
My dear Sir
I am happy that you have esctricated yourself from your old embanasononts__ I will always be happy to have the power equal to my milination to serve you_
I have been confined with asevere attack of the rheumatisms but am gettin better.
Hry Knox & his little 
family
2.208
family are well_  The children have had the measels, but all of them went through the disorder tolerably well.
Mrs K unites with me in [indecipherable] our coridal affection to Mrs Biddle and your children. 
I am my [indecipherable]
Your sincerely
HKnox
[Editors Note: Henry Knox signs it with the H initial of his first name and the K initial of his last name intertwined.]
