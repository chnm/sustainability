description: Informs the committee that Major Tousard has submitted his plans and estimates for the defenses of Baltimore centered on Whetstone Point [eventually Fort McHenry]; tells them that the national
  funds allotted for this purpose are $20,000, while the estimate is for around $30,000.  If the plan is approved, McHenry expects the citizens of Baltimore to make up the difference, by either monetary
  contributions or labor.  Feeling that he cannot order the commencement of the fort without the city's willingness to submit to these conditions, he has placed the $20,000 and the power to commence construction
  at the disposal of the city.  He has also ordered the commandant of Fort McHenry [apparently the posting was established before the fort was constructed] to detach construction details to work on the
  fortifications.
