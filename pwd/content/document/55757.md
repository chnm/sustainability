description: McHenry, on behalf of President Washington, advises all the Indians assembled to not go to war with each other.  The representatives of the tribes then comment on this advice and on peace-making
  among themselves; the loss of land, the fate of Indian prisoners held by other tribes, and the shared use of hunting grounds are discussed.  Much is made of the union of all the Indian nations as brothers,
  as is the obeying of United States laws.  Many of the Indians ask that the words spoken here be remembered, and McHenry promises to convey their words to the president.
