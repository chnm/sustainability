Philada. June 22d. 1786.

D. Sir

After I returned from N York Col, DeHaas was so ill that I could not see him, and soon after died, a few days after he was buried I called on his son, from whom I obtained a copy of the Payrolls and my receipts, by which it appears I have not received the whole amount of the half pay pr month I should have received.

Mr DeHaas seems to decline giving me a Certificate until Mr Shattas settles his father's accounts, which I fear will be attended with delay  I likewise found the account I had rendered him for settlement of the stoppages, which agrees with the one I left you, except one line in the credit side, which is mentioned for October, was from a former pay Roll vizt. June 30:th. to Septemr 30:th  I shall be much obliged to you to inform me of every voucher that may be necessary, or whether these copies I have already obtained will be sufficient  I have purchased some City Lotts for Certificates at public sale and shall be under the necessity of getting a settlement as soon as possible your friendly assistance will be ever remembered  Please to write me as soon as convenient.

Yours &c

Wm Williams

Cap. Howell.
