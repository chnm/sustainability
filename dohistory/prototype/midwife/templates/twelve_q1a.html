The documents used in historical research can take many forms. In this instance, the words on paper money used during Martha Ballard's lifetime point to the meaning of some numbers in the diary: '12/' meant twelve shillings.

During the American Revolution many states which had been using English money started issuing their own paper money, partly as a step toward independence from England but also because the War caused copper, nickel, and gold to become scarce. To correct the shortage, states issued paper money in all denominations. At the same time, paper money from the Continent was circulating (some of it conterfeit) which eventually led to devaluation, and social and financial chaos.

The devaluation of Continental and state issued paper money had a big impact on life in America. To restore confidence in using paper money the Constitution of the United States prohibited states from issuing it. But it was not until the advent of centralized banking that the financial turmoil in America began to stabilize.
