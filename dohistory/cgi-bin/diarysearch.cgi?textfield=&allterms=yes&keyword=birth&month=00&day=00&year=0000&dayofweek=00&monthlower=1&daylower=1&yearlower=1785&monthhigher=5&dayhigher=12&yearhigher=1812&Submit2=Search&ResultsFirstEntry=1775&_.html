 April 16, 1799, Tuesday

view full Diary page

 

X. Birth mr Stickneys 4th Son 6th Child. XX.

Clear and warm. I was Calld at 8h morn to magr Stickneys wife in Labour. they Conducted me over the river in a Sleigh, pusht by him and mr Eaton. I was Drawn from mr Devenports Landing by a hors and Sleigh. the Patient was Safe Deld at 7h and 30m Evng of a fine Son and is Clevy. my Self, and the Ladys who assisted, tarried all night. Henry Badcock here after me ys night, I was absent.   at mag Stickneys. Birth 15th. recd 9/ as A reward. Crost by ice.  
 

 

 April 17, 1799, Wednesday

view full Diary page

 

X. Birth Ths Stickneys Son. XX.

Cloudy. mr Thomas Stickney Calld us from his Brothers to See his wife who was in Labour. Shee was Safe Deld of her 3d Son at 6h pm and is Cleverly. mrss Devenport and the mrss Whites were Conducted home. I tarried all night. mrss Conrey is Nurs to this Patient.   at Leut Thos. Stickneys. Birth 16th. recd 9/ as a rewd.  
 

 

 April 24, 1799, Wednesday

view full Diary page

 

X. Birth Daniel Heartfds 3d Dagt. XX.

Snowd. I was Calld at 2h morn to See mrss Heartford who was in Labour, her marm was there. mrss Haywood & Bell were Calld and Shee was Safe Deld of a fine Dagt at 6h, I returnd home at 2 PM in a Sleigh. mrss Heartford had two Childn Born ye 20th of June last.   at mr Heartfords. Birth 17th. I receivd 12/ as a rewd.  
 

 

 April 27, 1799, Saturday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear forenn, Cloudy aftern, Snowd before night. mr Heartford Calld me about 2h pm to See his wife who has a very ill turn. her marm went with us, we batht her and used Such remedies as gave her relief. I returnd home at Evn. mrs Sewal Sleeps here.   at mr Heartfords, his wife was very ill but is Some Easier.  
 

 

 April 28, 1799, Sunday

view full Diary page

 

 Cloudy and very bad wriding. mr Ballard and Son Cyrus went to meeting. Becky Sewall wrode home on our hors at noon. Ephm here at Evn. I am informd that mrss Heartford is Cleverly.   at home. mrs Sewall went from here at nn. 
 

 

 April 29, 1799, Monday

view full Diary page

 

X. Birth Jessy Kimbals Dagt. XX.

Cloudy. I was Calld at 2h morn to go and See the wife of Jessy Kimbal who was in her Labour. I went to the Brick yard of Norcross on foot, then by water. we arivd where the patient was at 4, and Shee was Deld of her 3d Dagt and 4th Child at 5. I, after necesary Performances, returnd home at 9h morn.   at Jessy Kimbals. Birth 18th. recd 9/ as a reward.  
 

 

 April 30, 1799, Tuesday

view full Diary page

 

X. Birth Stutely Springers Son & 7th Child. XX.

Clear & Showers by turns. mrss Capen & Dagt Lambard here, Moses Partridges wife & Lidia Densmore allso. mr Ballard been to work on his house. I was Calld at Sun Sett to go to See the wife of Stutely Springer who is in Labour. I arivd there after Dusk in Evn.   this was the 29th at Springers. Clear, my Patient was Safe Deld at 5h m of a fine Son. I Sett out for home after Breakfast. Calld to See mrss Ball, find her very Low with a Soar Breast. Spoke with Dagt Ballard, Le faett has a Sweling on his throat. mr Ballard is up there to work, Cyrus Carting wood, Sally washing, Jason Livermore Came home last Evng. at Stutely Springers. Birth 19th. the inft wd 9 lb. recd fee at Hamln Store.  
 

 

 May 2, 1799, Thursday

view full Diary page

 

 Cloudy and Clear by turns, raind at night. the above Should have been wrote for this day, rain this night. Cloudy morn, Cleard at 9h morn. Son Town went to See his Father who is very unwell, he Calld on his return home. mrs Harington here, brot 14_ yds Cloath her mother wove for me. Isaac Hardins wife here, paid me all Shee owd me for Service I had Done her. I was Calld at 11h Evng to go to See the wife of Levy Page, found her in Labour, was there all night.   at home, Son Town here. at Levy Pages at night.  
 

 

 May 3, 1799, Friday

view full Diary page

 

 Cloudy part of this Day. I went from Levy to James Pages to See lheir Child who has a bad humour on the head and feet. my Patient had her women Calld after Dinner. we were up with her the most of the night. the women which attended were mrss Hains, Cumings & Briggs.   at mr Pages.  
 

 

 May 4, 1799, Saturday

view full Diary page

 

X. Birth Levy Pages Dagt & 4th Child. XX.

Clear and Pleast. mrss Pages illness Came on more Severe at Day and Shee was Safe Deld of a Dagt, her 4th Child, at 7h morn. I left her and infant as well as Could be Expected and returnd as far as Son Lambards. mr Ballard Calld and Conducted me home. we are informd of the Deaths of mr Rhuebin Page of Sidney Belgrade and mr Soper of Pitts ton, and old mr Hastings, winthrop.   at Ditos. 20th Birth. recd 6/. Jany 15th recd 3/. Acount of Deaths.  
 

 

 May 5, 1799, Sunday

view full Diary page

 

X. Birth Theops Hamlins 3d Son. XX.

Clear and Cold. I was Calld at 2h ys morn to go and See the wife of mr Theops Hamlin who was in Labour with her Sixth Child. I wrode with mr John Rockwood, reacht there Safe alltho the roads were very bad, and Shee was Safe Deld at 3h morn of her 3d Son and Sixth Child. I left all Cleverly and Came home at 7. attended worship, my famely allso. Son and Dagt Lambd Came here after Service.   at mr Theophs Hamlins. Birth 21st. recd 12/ as a reward. Son & Dagt Lambd here.  
 

 

 May 8, 1799, Wednesday

view full Diary page

 

 rain and Sun Shine by turns. mr Ballard and Cyrus went to Son Pollards to the puting up his house fraim. I wint to Son Lambards. Calld to See mrss Densmore, find her very feeble. I Sent Polly Hodges to mr W Waids to Carry a memorandum to mrss Haywood to procure Some things for me at Boston.   at Son Lambards. I Sent 9 Dols by mrss Haywood to Boston.  
 

 

 May 10, 1799, Friday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear part of the day. mr Ballard & I attended Lecture. Dagt Lambard had a faint turn and left meeting. mr Ballard and I Calld there after meeting, Shee Complains of a pain in the head and Side. I Calld to See mrss Hamlin, find her Cleverly.   at Lecture and mr Hamlins and Son Lambards.  
 

 

 May 11, 1799, Saturday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear part of the day. I wed part of my Current trees. mr Vauhn Calld and took Some limbs of the white ones. mr Ballard been at his hous, workt about the Chimney. I have been at home.   at home. we had 14 lb Veal of mr Livermore.  
 

 

 May 12, 1799, Sunday

view full Diary page

 

Snowd this morn.

Showers. I went to meeting, and all my famely. the ordinance of ye Supper was administred. we Calld at Son Lambd s after Service and Dind. Mercy Tuper Came here to work for me this weak.   at meeting. Mercy Tuper Came here to Spin for me.  
 

 

 May 19, 1799, Sunday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear. mr Ballard went to Pittstn to See Bror Town who, Cyrus informs, is near the Close of life. may God be his guide thro the valley of the Shaddow of death, may the Holy Angels Stand waiting to receive and Conduct his departing Spirit to the Relms of immortal bliss!!! Cyrus informd me at intermition that mrss Gill is Calld to Go and See her Father whom it is Expected will not Survive long, Shee tarries there this night.   At home. mr Ballard has been to See Bror Town, he left him alive but very low. Son & Dagt Lambd and mrs Smart here at Evng. 
 

 

 May 21, 1799, Tuesday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear and windy. mr Ballard went to Son Jon as and Sowd flax. mrss Dingley here, Presented me two Dols for assisting her in her illness last Nover. I planted Squashes.   at home. mrss Dingley here.  
 

 

 May 23, 1799, Thursday

view full Diary page

 

 [    ] rainy forn, Cleard up at 1h pm. we went to Pittstn and attended the interment of the remains of our Brother, Thos Town. Sons Town and Jon a Dind here and went with us. mr Gill and wife went on before us. the Corps were Born to the Church where Prayers were red, than Carried into the Church yard and interd. we Calld at mr Moors and took Tea, arivd at home at Sun Sett. Son Town Sleeps here.   at the Funeral of Bror Town.  
 

 

 May 26, 1799, Sunday

view full Diary page

 

 Cloudy. Cyrus Came up from pittstn. mr Ballard, he and Sally at meeting all day. I attended aftern. Sally did not return till Evng. we Calld at Son Lambds, they are well.   at meeting.  
 

 

 May 27, 1799, Monday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear morn but Cool. mr Ballard Set out to the meddow Early, Cyrus to pittstn, Sally washing. Allin Lambd Came here. A person Calld on me to go and See friend Nathan Husseys wife at Varsalboro, but he thought it advisable to go on for Doct Parker. I was Calld to See mrss Burtun before noon, find her unwell and tarried all night. Shee got better.   At mr Burtuns.  
 

 

 May 28, 1799, Tuesday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear and pleasant. I went in to See mrss Stone and mrss Miller, then Sett out for home. Calld at mr Whitwells and Lambd s. mr Ballard been to Son Jon as.   at Dittoes. Bulah and Childn Sleep here.  
 

 

 May 30, 1799, Thursday

view full Diary page

 

X. Birth James Burtuns 2nd Dagt. XX.

Clear part of the Day. mr Ballard to the Meddow, finisht mending the fence. I was Calld 7h pm to See mrss Burtun who was in Labour, and was Deld at 10 Evn of a fine Daugtr. I tarried thro the night. mr Fenny was there at 8, wisht me to go and See his wife who was ill allso, but Could not leave mrss Burtun to Speak with hime, he went after mrss Mosier, Shee was Absent.   at mr Burtuns. Birth 22nt. recd 12/ out of his Store.  
 

 

 June 8, 1799, Saturday

view full Diary page

 

 rainy. I was Calld at 4h Pm by my Son Pollard to go and See his wife who is unwell. I wrode from mr Robinsons alone, reacht my Sons before night. it raind most powerfully at night. I found my Self to be after the manner of woman this night. [1806]. I have remaind in that Situation Every [moment] Since.   at Son Pollards.  
 

 

 June 13, 1799, Thursday

view full Diary page

 

X. Birth Son Pollards first Son & 4th Child. XX.

Clear Part of the Day. my Dagt was Siesd with her Labour and her women were Calld and Shee was Safe Deld (at 12 oClock) of a fine Son, her 4th Child, and is as well as Could be Expected. her husband returnd at Evn and Seems much pleased with the favour. may God Bless them and their ofspring.   at Son Pollards. Birth 23rd. recd 6/. recd a letter from Sister Williams of May 30.  
 

 

 June 14, 1799, Friday

view full Diary page

 

 rainy. I have been with my Dagt, Shee remaind Cleverly till Evn when Shee was in great Pain by her milk Coming. I Sett up with her till midnight. Sally Selvester tended her the remainder of the night. Shee got Some rest, the infant Seems well. we Sent for Sist Williams to Come and nurs but did not obtain her. mr Pollard did not Come home.   at Ditoes.
