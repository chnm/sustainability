War Department 28 February 1797.
Sir
I have examined the claim you have referred for
extra pay, as supernumerary aid to the late Commander in Chief, and
find, that the duties which the Commander had to perform, including
the almost constant calls upon him by some one or other of the Indian
Nations, down to the time of decease, required additional aids, and
that for this it becomes a proper charge against the United States; but
inasmuch as the appropriations for the military department have
been exhausted, and it has not been included in the estimate render-
ed from this department, it may be advisable to remove any doubts 
that might be raised on the subject or have it referred to Congress.
I am, Sir
respectfully,
Your obedt. Serv:
James McHenry
Capain Thomas Lewis
5 Cong. 1 sess.
