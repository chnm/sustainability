Ruth Strohl Ansbach: She [my mother] just finally said to him, "I'm getting a job." And she went up and she got the job, and she came back, and she said, "I'm working." He [my father] didn't like it too much. I remember it was hunting season. The first day of hunting season was her first day to go to work. And you could tell he didn't like it too much, but I think he kind of figured there would be no money coming in until he would get his Black Lung. They were collecting unemployment, but at that time then it wasn't enough either, you still had other bills and stuff to pay. But I can remember my sister and I saying, "Just go, just go and get the job, don't worry about what he says!" And she did it, she said, "I'm not doing it this time." She said, "I went through that the last time, worrying about the bills." Because it was always her that paid the bills, you know, he would just give her the check and she took care of all the financial things. And then she'd always be moaning, "Well, yeah but it's me that has the headache; you don't worry about it. You don't worry about it till I say, 'Well, I can't give you this or we can't get that,' then it's like, 'Well, why can't we get it?'" She was the one who took care of the money. . .
