description: Establishes a uniform principle of what outfitting actions taken by belligerent European ships in American ports are illegal. Any alterations to guns or creation of new carriages or portholes
  are illegal. Describes how a British privateer was made to conform to these rules. To keep neutrals from outfitting privateers for belligerents, these bans are extended to all vessels. Declares that American
  ships are neutral, and should not be outfitted in similar fashions unless they can prove the necessity of such preparations. Encloses a further rule on privateers sailing out of U.S. ports and a Congressional
  act for punishing crimes [neither is included].
