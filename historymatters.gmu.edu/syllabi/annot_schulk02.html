My approach to teaching United States history involves the use of popular music and popular films as a means of stimulating student interest in the subject matter. Each piece of music, film or film clip is carefully chosen for its serious academic content. On the first day of class, I play the popular song "Only in America" by Jay and the Americans as a way of introducing the issue of whether the United States has been and continues to be a "land of opportunity." I also have the students view the opening scenes of the film Nine to Five as a prelude to a discussion of both sex discrimination and economic opportunity in recent years.
