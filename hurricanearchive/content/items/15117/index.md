Allison Pena of the National Park Service and Evelyn Minor, in front of a painting of Fazedeville as it appeared prior to 1964. Of Fazendeville, Evelyn Minor says, \"I see the village in my mind when I go to the [Chalmette Batteground] park.  Like others, the vision fills me with sorrow.  I visualize it exactly as it use to be.  I see a very quiet serene little place where people used to live.  Where are we going to get a place like that now?\"  -- Evelyn Rose Francis Minor\r\n\r\nThe ancestors of Evelyn Minor\'s late husband, Clinton Joseph Minor, were long-time residents of Fazendeville. Evelyn Minor now lives with her family in Los Angeles.
