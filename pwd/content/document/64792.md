description: George Salmon surveys the impending Congressional elections of 1798 in Maryland for James McHenry.  Salmon notes that "party spirit rages every where and with great violence." He also says
  that "parties [presumably party politics] are beyond anything ever before known." According to Salmon, "the election begins next Monday and disagreeable consequences may be expected" in Maryland. Salmon
  calls Joshua Seney "Galatin the Second." Salmon is presumably an ardent Federalist and believes that the State of Maryland will not be represented in so proper manner as formerly.
