- desire peace with the six nations and all the world
- appearances of war with Great Britain are much lessened
- agents of discord are to be totally disregarded by the indians
- noting every occurrence of importance
- pacific appearance
- english
- learn the true state of the report of the British having established a fort at the rapids of the Miami
- garrisoned with three companies of troops
- money placed in charge of that gentleman for the public service under your orders
- dollars
- purpose of paying the charge of transportation and other public objects
- my letter
- goods forwarded to you by your son will give you the means of assembling the chiefs at a very short day
- make preparations so that they will be assembled by the time of the arrival of the goods
- endeavour to learn also the sentiments of your neighbours
- state of affairs in that quarter
- ascertain the real sentiments of the Six Nations with respect to the continuance of peace and the establishment of the settlement at Presque Isle
- hostilities rendered highly probable
- committed murders upon some people near Fort Franklin as mentioned in the Pittsburg paper
- first copy of the letter herein enclosed was transmitted by your son who left this city
- transportation of the goods mentioned in the list may retard his progress
- judged it necessary to forward this letter in express
- good understanding with the Six Nations upon the Allegheny seems to be in some measure interrupted
