Found: Martha's standard fee for deliveries in 1794 was 6/ (6 shillings).


To answer some historical questions, we need several sources or documents. In this case, keeping track of the fees Martha Ballard received in many diary entries shows that her standard fee stayed at six shillings for most of the years she worked as a midwife. Additional medicines or expenses (such as a horse to travel to the delivery) could mean a higher fee.

In most of the country in the decades after the American Revolution, actual cash remained scarce. In the Hallowell credit economy, this was true as well. Martha Ballard received some cash, but more often she received goods, services, or credit.

Martha Ballard's diary provides a unique perspective on the transition in medicine from the midwife-healer to physicians. Dr. Page's fee was greater than Martha's and this difference in pay was due to his status as a formally educated male with claims to 'scientific' and 'newly improved' methods and treatments.
