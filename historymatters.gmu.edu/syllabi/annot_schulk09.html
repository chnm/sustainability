Whenever time permits, I like to set aside one or two class periods to have students bring in music that they like to listen to which they believe has something significant to say about American society or American history. The only limitation is that students are not permitted to bring in and play pieces which contain lyrics which are obscene. Students are usually surprised by how many of the songs they listen to contain significant commentary or messages, and they are often equally astounded by the wide variety of musical tastes represented among their peers. Such class sessions help to build student interest and to reinforce the idea that serious study of popular culture can yield important insights into American history and values.
