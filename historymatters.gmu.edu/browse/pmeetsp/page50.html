Anarchy Archives: An Online Research Center on the History and Theory of Anarchism
Dana Ward, Department of Political Studies, Pitzer College.
This digital repository presents a wealth of anarchist literature and information on anarchists and anarchist movements around the world. Most of this material comes linked through notable anarchist thinkers. The websites Cynosure section presents 10 of these thinkers, including Emma Goldman, Peter Kropotkin, and Pierre-Joseph Proudhon (arguably the first to describe himself as an anarchist), and for each one includes a biography, a complete bibliography of their works and commentary on their works, and links (some onsite and some offsite) to many of their books, journals, letters, pamphlets and articles. Fifty-one bright but lesser anarchists are also featured, including Leo Tolstoy, Sacco and Vanzetti, and Ghandi. Information on and selections from thirty-five pamphlets and journals and periodicals, as well as multiple editions of six anarchist periodicals are also available. A selection of 38 periodicals from Argentina, some with accompanying descriptions and multiple editions digitized, is buried within the Periodicals section. To complement these sources, the website also includes information on worldwide anarchist and labor movements, including the Haymarket Massacre and the Paris Commune. A timeline and comprehensive bibliography provide additional context.
