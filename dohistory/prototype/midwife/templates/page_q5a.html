Found: evidence of Martha's thoughts about Dr. Page.


If we looked at only one of these diary entries about Dr. Page we would lack a complete understanding of the many ways that Martha Ballard's life and career intersected with Dr. Page's. The July 9, 1799 entry tells us that she purchased medicines and herbs from Dr. Page's shop. The June 15, 1797 and June 10, 1797 entries show that Martha Ballard was treated by Dr. Page: he set her broken bones. But the October 10, 1794 and July 10, 1796 entries suggest that she did not approve of Dr. Page's work in obstetrics. Still, according to the diary, Martha Ballard worked cooperatively with doctors for the most part.

Taken together, the entries provide insight into the larger issue of how the fields of midwife-healers and doctors were separated and how the new male medical establishment was slowly taking over the responsibilities traditionally allotted to the female midwife-healers. Martha Ballard thought Page was an upstart, but she also saw him as invading her turf.

Diaries are selective records written in a context. Oftentimes, cross- referencing the names appearing in them with other documents (e.g tax records, other diaries, church records, probate records) is required in order to understand who everyone is and how they fit into the world depicted in the diary. By looking at the opinions of a diarist against the backdrop of other documents, we acquire a more complex understanding of how the diarist fits into the community.
