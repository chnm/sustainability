<p>In an <a href="/document.php?id=42271">undated document</a>, Benjamin Thompson set forth the history of his life during the era of the American Revolution. Although born in Boston, he was apprenticed at the age of fourteen to a merchant in Montreal. He recounts his efforts on the part of the American cause, prior to his departure from Montreal in 1776, all in an effort to qualify for compensation under the Act of Congress for the Relief of Refugees from the British Provinces of Canada and Nova Scotia.</p>
<p>The first eight pages of this document have been transcribed, but the last four (images 9-12) have not. Can you <a href="/scripto/?documentId=42271&amp;pageId=77421">complete this transcription</a> so its content can become part of the searchable archive of the Papers of the War Department?</p>
