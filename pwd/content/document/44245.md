description: Letter from Creek Indian Agent James Seagrove to leader of Courtas [Courlas]. Reports that great and kind father General Washington, President of United States is pleased to hear of the peaceable
  conduct of his Creek children. Glad to hear the talks of upper towns continue good. Will be at Cussetah 1 May, where talk will be straight. Beware of the northern Indians giving bad talks. Chiefs of north
  see great U.S. Army and now want peace. Great father Washington will take pity on them and will not destroy them, though they have been very bad; deserve to be punished; but [Washington] is kind and merciful.
  Continue to give your people good talks; keep them straight. Mr Bernard will deliver this. Please assist and befriend him and Mr. Holmes. Pay attention to what they tell you.
