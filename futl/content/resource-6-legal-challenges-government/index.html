Going to Court in Arlington

In Arlington County, the local NAACP moved quickly after Brown II to register a small number of African American students in the district’s white schools. NAACP member Dorothy Hamm, featured in the video, attempted to register her son, Leslie, and two other students at Stratford Junior High. As the video states, these students were turned away.

In 1957, in response to the Brown ruling, Virginia Governor Thomas Stanley threatened to close any Virginia schools that allowed desegregation. The Arlington Board of Education did not oppose desegregation, but was not willing to risk school closure.

The NAACP and civil rights activists realized that further actions by the courts would be needed to desegregate Arlington schools. Meanwhile, the state of Virginia began to pursue a policy, led by powerful Senator Harry Byrd, to defend segregation at all costs.
