Found: evidence that indicates when Martha died.


You began this investigation with an abridged version of Dr. Ben Page's detailed and lengthly obituary/tribute. In contrast, we see that the terse obituary written for Martha Ballard does not even mention her work as a healer and midwife, or even the exact day of her death. Instead, she is identified as Ephraim's Ballard's 'consort' or wife.

Surprisingly, we couldn't even be sure her given name was Martha without the diary. When she married she lost her both her surname and given name and became known as Mrs. Ballard. In 1830, Reverend Tappin, the minister who Martha tells us in her last diary entry "made A Prayer adapted to my Case" in the days before her death in 1812, began recording the names of past and present female members of the church. He wrote "Dorothy Ballard" next to Ephraim Ballard's name. James North, the town historian, referred to her as Mrs. Ephraim Ballard and occasionally as "Hannah" (the name of Martha's daughter). Fortunately, she wrote her own name in the back cover of one of her many diary booklets.
