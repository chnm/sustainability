The President John F. Kennedy Assassination Records Collection
National Archives and Records Administration.
On order of an Act of Congress signed into law in 1992, the National Archives has gathered federally-created material relating to the 1963 assassination of President John F. Kennedy—including declassified material from every relevant presidential commission, congressional investigation, and executive-branch agency—and placed it in a single collection, supplemented with materials donated by local governments, presidential libraries, institutions, and private individuals. While original records are not available online, this site presents a searchable database and extensive finding aids and includes full texts of the reports issued by the Warren Commission, published in 1964, and the 1979 House Select Committee on Assassinations. This is a well-designed site that will be of most value to scholars of the assassination who plan to conduct research at the Archives.

The Nixon Links
Norman Nithman.
Presents an array of primary and secondary materials relating to Richard Nixon, focusing particularly on Watergate and other ignoble episodes from the Nixon administration. Offers 11 links to recent news stories and essays by CNN, the Washington Post, and other news outlets; the texts of 14 Nixon speeches from 1947 to 1974, including his resignation speech, the “Checkers Speech” and his first and second inaugural addresses; and links, brief notes, and other items relating to 11 “friends of Dick,” including Patrick Buchanan, Chuck Colson, Gerald R. Ford, H. R. Haldeman, and E. Howard Hunt. Also gives a special presentation, “The World of Spiro Agnew,” as well as 43 additional links to general resources on the Nixon regime. A solid introduction that incorporates material from the recently released "Nixon tapes."
