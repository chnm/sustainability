<p>Parkehyde, one of our volunteer transcribers recently finished a document that highlights a frequent situation in the early War Department. A Captain Bliss had evidently asked for an accounting of money due him. After a check of the relevant paperwork, however, the Commissioner of Army Accounts determined that Captain Bliss was not owed any funds. Furthermore, the letter advises Bliss that it could be a waste of his time to pursue the matter further, although, even in the eighteenth century, he was free to speak with counsel. It is at the same time a personal story, a bureaucratic story, and a military one.</p>
