HOW DO YOU READ A DOCUMENT LIKE "BARTLEBY"?

I come from the discipline of literature. And I spend a surprising amount of time, even at this late date, reading it. That is to say, reading it, re-reading it. It is always something of a trial, a mystery to me how much time it takes, even though theoretically you have the competence. But it's a matter of sitting there reading, re-reading, and doing some very elemental, non-cultural analysis things, first at least. Even a supposedly highly competent reader, someone like me, who's done this a lot, repeats again and again something of the experience of the amateur. In other words, I come to a text with excitement and a certain amount of terror because I don't always get it. Or I don't understand enough of it. Or I come to understand something new this time. Sometimes the new thing overturns what I thought before.
