The 1915 film The Birth of a Nation was directed by D.W. Griffith and based largely on a melodramatic novel and play, both titled The Clansman, written by Thomas Dixon. In the second half of the film, set in the period following the Civil War, the film depicts southern blacks as vicious and lascivious; their northern white allies as cunning, unscrupulous, and arrogant; and the southern white characters as suffering repeated political and sexual indignities at the hands of white northerners and black southerners before being rescued by the gallant, hooded riders of the Ku Klux Klan. The National Association for the Advancement of Colored People, an advocacy group for African Americans, mounted a protest campaign and attempted to have the film banned. Dixon said that the goal of his fiction was "to teach the North, the young North, what it has never known--the awful suffering of the white man during the dreadful Reconstruction period. I believe that Almighty God anointed the white men of the South by their suffering during that time . . . to demonstrate to the world that the white man must and shall be supreme."

Black filmmaker Oscar Micheaux was a pioneer of early twentieth-century challenges to stereotyped Hollywood depictions of African Americans. He produced over thirty independent feature films during his career, most of which were distributed to movie theatres in African-American neighborhoods. His 1919 film Within Our Gates was a melodrama that told the story of heroine Sylvia Landry and her attempts to start a school for African Americans in the South; the film included a powerful lynching scene. Some film historians believe that Micheaux deliberately patterned his attempted rape scene after the scene in The Birth of a Nation. Released in January 1920, the film drew protests from both black and white audiences and exhibitors because of fears that it would incite race riots like those experienced in cities around the country during the previous summer of 1919.

While The Birth of a Nation was celebrated as a masterpiece, Within Our Gates was lost until a print was discovered in Spain in the 1970s and returned to the Library of Congress.
