 September 25, 1796, Sunday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear. Cyrus, Ebenz, & Patty attinded public worship foren. I went my Self aftern. Doct Cony Calld to See me this morn, he informd me that mr Waids infant Dagt Expired ys morn. its Death was Causd by the hooping Cough which brot on fitts. I Calld at Son Lambarts after meeting, Cyrus allso. we took Tea with them. mr Savage informd me that mr Ballard was unbe unwell when he arivd at the uper Settlement. God grant the restoration of health to him if yet wanting and return him & his assistants in Safety to their respective homes.   at meeting & at Son Lambarts. Death of mr Waids infant AE 31 Days.  
 

 

 September 26, 1796, Monday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear, the wind Northerd. I attended Funeral of mr Waids infant. I Calld at mr Densmores as I went, at mr Dingleys as I returnd. mrs Thwing and her Sister Nancy Kidder, mrss Dingley, Heartford and mrs Nancy Hilton took Tea with me.   at the funeral of mrs Waids inft. mrs Foster is unwell. mrs Thwing here.  
 

 

 October 1, 1796, Saturday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear. I went after Breakfast to Son Lambarts, they were Seting out for to go to mr Capins. I assisted them to Dress & See them Depart, then went to Son Pollards, they are well Except little Hannah. Shee is better than Shee was. I Came home near night. Cyrus & Ebenz housd our flax. Cyrus then went to the hook, bot at F Browns & Stratns _ gln Spirit & 1 lb Coffee, paid Cash 6/. Son Ephm Came to Sleep here.   at Sons Pollards & Lambts, the latter went to Varsalboro.  
 

 

 October 2, 1796, Sunday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear. my famely, all Except my Self, attended worship all Day. I Did aftern, & Calld to See Shubal Pittss wife. Shee was faint and went out of meeting. Son Ephm Came and Supt with me and returnd home.   at meeting & at Shubal Pittss. 
 

 

 October 3, 1796, Monday

view full Diary page

 

X. Birth Antony Brackets Son. XX.

Clear Part of the Day, rain at night. I have Swept my Chambers & lower rooms, & Done other matters. Pally mended me a quillt. I was Calld to See the wife of Anthony Bracket at 9h Evn, was fatagud with my hors & the bad roads. Shee was Deld of a Son at 11. I tarried all night, mother and infant are as well as Can be Expected.   at Anthony Bracketts.  
 

 

 October 4, 1796, Tuesday

view full Diary page

 

X. Birth Anton y Bracketts Son. XX.

A rainy Day. I left my patients Cleverly after Breakfast and Came to Son Jonas; a very heavy Shower while I was there. my Son Conducted me as far as ye meeting house where we met with mr Dingly, who Desird me to go & See his wife who is very unwell. I Calld there, Shee Seems Some better. I arivd at home at Dusk, feel fatagud.   at Ditoes & Son Jonas & mr Dingleys. Birth 47. receivd 9/ as a reward.  
 

 

 October 11, 1796, Tuesday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear. I went to See mrss Child. mrss Dingley went to Son Pollards with me. I Calld at mr Hamlins to See his Dady who lately left Oxford. my friends are well there, he informs yt John Wolcott Cutt his own Throat just before he left there.   at mr Childs & other friends.  
 

 

 October 13, 1796, Thursday

view full Diary page

 

Birth John Chamberlins Son.

Clear, a Cool morn. I was Calld at 3h morn to go to John Chamberlins. I was attended by Saml Jackson, arivd at 7h. my Patients illness Came on rapidly 6h pm and Shee was Deld at about 7 of a fine Son. I tarried all night. Josiah Norcross Infant interd this Day.   at John Chamberlins. Birth 48th. mr Ballard returnd [home] from his [    ] this Day.  
 

 

 October 15, 1796, Saturday

view full Diary page

 

 Cloudy Some part of the day. I was Calld to See Grifins youngest Child, it Seems very unwell. I returnd and a mrss Austin Came to me for Some advice, Shee being not So well as Shee Could wish. my Dagts were here yesterday, mrss Pitts allso. I Sent to mr Ezekiel Pages for Patty Easty to Come here. Shee Came and Confist that Shee took our property in Cash twice the Sumer past, and likewise promist to make restoration. the matter is not yet Setled. God grant Shee may be led to declare the truth and, if posible, make restitution to all whome Shee has injured of their property.   at home Grefins to See a Sick Child. a mrss Austin here for advice in Case of illness.  
 

 

 October 16, 1796, Sunday

view full Diary page

 

 Cloudy Day, raind at night. mr Ballard and the young men went to meeting at the fort, the Girls to the hook. I tarried at home. mr Georg Brown & Phillip Bullin Dind with us. I have been at home, Did not feel So well as usual. I had a very Severe fitt of ye Cramp last night. mr Ballard & ye Girls rose to my assistance.   at home. Georg Brown & P Bullin Dind here.  
 

 

 October 22, 1796, Saturday

view full Diary page

 

 raind Some. I was Calld by master Dilingham to See his wife at 2h morn. Shee was ill thro the Day & night, the Case preter natural. I gave her three Small Dozes of Stiptick pouders which gave relief.   at mr Dilinghams.

 October 24, 1796, Monday

view full Diary page

 

X. Birth mr Pillsburys Dagt. XX.

Cloudy. I was Calld at 6h morn to See mrss Pillsbury who was in Labour. Shee was Safe Deld at 1h pm of a fine Dagt. I went from there to Doct Colmans Store, bot 2 oz Spermaseta @ /4_ /9. Do Cammomile. yd vial & Salts, & Box Snuf /8. from there to See mrss Child, find her better. I returnd and took Tea, receivd 6/ and then Came home, attended by mr Pittss Boy. I left mother and infant Cleverly.   at mr Pillsburys & Childs. Birth 50th. receivd 6/ as a reward. EbenZ [Cool] Left here.  
 

 

 November 3, 1796, Thursday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear. I have been at home assisting about my work. Becky began to weav my Coverled; it did not work according to my wish. we Calld my Dagt Lambart here, who gave instruction how to weav ym and we were able to perform. mr EZekiel Pages wife Calld here.   at home. Dagt Lambart here.  
 

 

 November 4, 1796, Friday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear and very pleasant. mr Ballard and my Self attended Lecture at ye meeting house. there were but a Small number there. I Calld to See my Dagts Childn who have the Cough, they are no better as yet. a young man by the name of Rice Sleeps here. Becky got my Coverled out and Shee and Patty went to mr Dingleys at Evng.   at Lecture, mr Pillsburys, Son Pollards & Lambarts.  
 

 

 November 6, 1796, Sunday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear and pleast Day, Some rain at Evng. my famely all attended worship at the fort. the Ordinance of the Super Administred. mr Ballard and I went to mr Childs at intermition. we Calld at Son Lambarts after meeting, the Dear little Babe has the Cough yet, as Severe as Ever.   at meeting & mr Childs & Son Lambarts. Son Ephm Came and Sleeps here.  
 

 

 November 7, 1796, Monday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear. mr Ballard & Cyrus went to Town went to Meeting. mr Capin Calld here. Phillip Bullin Sleeps here. mrs Dingley & Nancy here to Tea. Thos Kenny Sent me 6/ by my Son Cyrus. I have been at home helping do house work & knitting.    
 

 

 November 13, 1796, Sunday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear and pleast. mr Ballard & Cyrus attended worship, Polly and my Self have Colds and did not. mr Lambt & wife and the Babe here after meeting. mrss Rice Sent for me to See her, Shee being very ill. mr Ballard Conducted me there. I gave her Melleot Tea which brot on perspiration ang gave releaf and I returnd. Calld to See Son Pollards Childn who are unwell.   at mr Rices. Son & Dagt Lambart here.  
 

 

 November 14, 1796, Monday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear and very Pleast. revd mr Stone Calld on us and Dind. Pally Easty Came and restord 11/2 of in part for of the money Shee took from us. Shee allso left a mustin gown for to Secure me till Shee restores the rest. I went this aftern to See mrss Rice and find her more Comfortable, Dagt Pollards Childn are better allso.   at [Elijah] Hinklys.  
 

 

 November 15, 1796, Tuesday

view full Diary page

 

X. Birth Elijah Hinkleys Dagt. XX.

Cloudy. mrss Hinkley was Safe Deld of a Dagt, her first Born, at 6h morn. I left her and inft as well as Could be Expected after Breakfast. receivd 9/ for my own Service & 1/6 for the hors. I went to bed and had Some Sleep, was Calld about noon to See mrss Byerns. it raind and I had great falague in geting there, find her very unwell. her women were there but She got more Comfortable and they all retird to their own homes.   at mr Hinkleys & mrss Burnss. Birth 50th. receivd 9/ as fee & 1/6 for hors.  
 

 

 November 17, 1796, Thursday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear. I went from mrss Byrnss to Old mr Whites, my patient is not well yet.   at Ditoes & at mr Whites.  
 

 

 November 19, 1796, Saturday

view full Diary page

 

 Cloudy. I left mrss Byrns not So well as Could be wisht, Came home attended by mr Graves; find my famely well. I was Calld at 6h evn to mr Partridges to See his Dear little Child who Mc Causland had Struk Down with a Billit of wood, and its life was Dispared of. I find it a little revivd. I Spent the night there.   at Ditoes and at mr Partridges to See his Child who mc Causland had almost Deprivd of life.  
 

 

 November 23, 1796, Wednesday

view full Diary page

 

 haild. I was Calld to See mrss Dingley at 3h morn, Shee had an Excessive pain in her head, and Severe Pukeing. we gave her a Decoction of [Cherytreoine] which relievd her. mr Bedinton dind here.   at mr Dingleys.  
 

 

 November 24, 1796, Thursday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear and pleast, but Cold. the ice is Stopt in the river. mr Abram Page broke thro and crost with his Canoe. I went to See mrs Dingley, find her Cleverly. mrs Densmore Came there. I was Calld from there to See Anna Partrage who had Convulsed turns, but is revivd. I Sett up till after 1 O Clock.   at Ditoes and at mr Partriges.  
 

 

 November 25, 1796, Friday

view full Diary page

 

X. Birth Saml Damrins Dagt. XX.

Clear morn, Cloudy Day. I was Calld at 2h 15m ys morn from mr Partridges to See the wife of Saml Damerin who was in Labour. Shee was Safe Deld of a Dagt, her first born Child at 10h. I left Mother & infant Cleverly & arivd at home at 1h 30 munits.   at mr Damrins. Birth 51st. receivd 9/ as a reward. at mrs Byrnss.
