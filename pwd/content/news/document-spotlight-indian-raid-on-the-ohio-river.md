<p>Recently, transcriber Brendawarneka transcribed a document in which Charles Johnston recounts an attack from hostile Native Americans.</p>
<p>Johnston gave a military deposition detailing the raid by some 54 Cherokees and Shawnees. In the attack, two people–a man and a woman–were killed and many more taken captive. The attackers also confiscated property.</p>
<p>Although short–only a dozen lines or so–the document provides a fascinating (and terrifying) look at life on the frontier in 1790.</p>
