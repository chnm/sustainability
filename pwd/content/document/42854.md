I have also sent [ripped edge] 1/2 of corn which I [illegible] to you to order it taken out immediately that Canoe may return One of the hands that I engaged has got Drunk and has wretchedly retained me O the Depravity of man!



Upside down:
Col. Hodgdon QMG
Fort Washington
Letter from Jn Smith, dated
1 May 1792
Recd 1 May
Sir
Last Evening I received your favour and now In part answer your request as to the Date when the horses returned  I find to Be the 19 of Feb - 5 bushel of potatoes a few eggs a small quantity of Butter [*] a half Dozen of fowls & a Lamb word comes the Sime [editor: word?] I fear at present I cannot getBut if you devsi [hole in paper] to Engage it in to me three weeks probably I can get it in the course of that time
I am Sir with much Esteem your most obedient servant 
J. Smith
May the 1 1792
N.B. I thought it best to have the fowl & lamb killed as they were not sent Last night but unfortunately I found myself mistaken about the quality of the Lamb when I came to have it butchered it turned out not as fat as it was represented to me. However if you Don't like it please send it back.

 I would have sent more but could not get it to please me.
