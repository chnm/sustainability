The map on the left, showing election results by county, makes it appear that George W. Bush won the election by a large margin, since so much of the map is red. The map on the right, showing election results by state, is also dominated by red, and also makes it appear that Bush won handily. But the map on the left does not indicate the number of registered voters per county, and so does not accurately reflect the number of votes cast. The map on the right does not indicate the relative strength of each state in the nation's electoral college system, which translates (sometimes imperfectly) the popular vote for president into the final result. Both of these factors--numbers of registered voters per county and number of electoral votes per state--are tied to population density.

In fact, while Bush won the election, the majority of voters cast their ballots for Al Gore: Bush received 50,456,062 votes and 271 electoral votes, and Gore received 50,996,582 votes and 266 electoral votes.
