  it into barrels. If there were any way to avoid hiring a cooper, Price would not ask to hire one, but there is no man at his post who knows how to set a hoop. Price believes that if he can start by the
  15th and the weather is dry the business will be done by November. He sends returns of ordnance and quartermasters stores for May care of Timothy Ketchum.
