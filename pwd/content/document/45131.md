description: From Savannah, James Seagrove addresses the Kings and Chiefs of the Cussetahs and all other friends of United States in lower Creek Towns. Some of those Indians detained at Seagrove's house
  have since run away. Expresses disappointment that they left. They were well taken care of.  The remaining detainees Seagrove sends via Mordecai and Townsend as a demonstration of good faith. Speaks of
  death of David Cornell. Informs Chiefs of meeting planned for 10 September 1793 at Oakmulgee. This meeting would serve to convince President of United States General George Washington of the peaceful
  intentions of his dutiful children. Warns chiefs to refrain from mischief in order to keep the Georgia militia from doing harm against them. Prevent the bad people from doing mischief.
