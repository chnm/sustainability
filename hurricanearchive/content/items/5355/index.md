My husband Tommy has an inkling Katrina may turn eastward a bit and make landfall at Biloxi Mississippi. While we woudn\'t wish this storm on anyone, New Orleans is the worst possible city to take a direct hit. Once in our hurricane history, we\'ve had a powerful storm dissipate - so we are all praying for that, even though it doesn\'t seem likely. \r\n\r\nAt any rate, Tommy thinks we won\'t see much action in Baton Rouge - and that New Orleans is going to be spared. It is a little bit of a let down when you work yourself up to such an adrenaline rush and don\'t even get a drop of rain. The media gets all pumped up and gets us all pumped up, but they really have to. When you have a million people at risk in you have to prepare for any possibility. So we\'ll see. \r\n\r\nOn another note, Nowadays we have advanced technologies but it hasn\'t always been so in my lifetime. Back in the day we relied only on the hurricane hunters who took the reconnaisance planes out and eyeballed the storms firsthand, then came back to report. We only had our local television stations to relay the information and you could pick up NOAA on the radio. No wealther channel or computers back then so the information we did receive was often hours behind the reality.
