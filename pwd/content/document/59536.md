  funds of Paymasters be refunded to the Treasurer of the United States.  The tenor of the letters was one of expediency.  Simmons requests the assistance of Heth in the dispersal of the letters to their
  recipients since Simmons did not know their place of residence or superscription. Also enclosed is a letter for Heth, in which he is reminded that he also stands charged for monies paid for same object.
