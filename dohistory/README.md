# DoHistory: Martha Ballard's Diary Online

> *"By itself, the diary is a puzzle. Martha Ballard was neither eloquent nor effusive. She often used one word or none at all when we wish for a thousand."*

DoHistory is an experimental, interactive case study that invites visitors to explore the process of piecing together the lives of ordinary people in the past. Based on the remarkable 200-year-old diary of midwife/healer Martha Ballard, this project demonstrates how historians work as detectives to uncover stories from fragments that have survived.

## Project Overview

Martha Ballard (1735-1812) was a Maine midwife who kept a diary faithfully for 27 years, from 1785 until just weeks before her death in 1812. Her great-great granddaughter preserved and donated the diary to the Maine State Library in 1930, where it remained largely ignored for fifty years until historian Laurel Thatcher Ulrich discovered it in 1982.

Ulrich's eight-year research project resulted in the Pulitzer Prize-winning book *A Midwife's Tale* (1990), which was later adapted into a documentary film. The overwhelming public interest in both the book and film inspired the creation of this website to share the detective work involved in historical research.

## Website Features

- **Complete Digital Diary**: All entries from Martha Ballard's handwritten diary (1785-1812) with both scanned images and transcribed text
- **Interactive Learning**: Two in-depth case studies demonstrating historical detective work
- **Primary Documents Archive**: Hundreds of supporting documents from the period
- **Educational Tools**: Resources for teachers, genealogists, and history enthusiasts
- **Magic Lens**: Interactive tool for examining diary entries
- **Search Functionality**: Full-text search across all diary entries and documents

## Technical Architecture

This is a static website built with late 1990s/early 2000s web technologies, but flattened in 2025 including the addition of static search:

- **Frontend**: HTML, JavaScript, CSS
- **Images**: Extensive use of image-based navigation elements and document scans
- **Structure**: Organized by diary years (1785-1812) with monthly subdivisions
- **Search**: Custom search implementation with template-based results

## Project History

- **February 4, 2000**: DoHistory website launched

## Preservation Note

**This project was flattened in 2025** as part of digital preservation efforts to maintain long-term access to this important early example of digital history. The static files preserve the complete interactive experience while ensuring future accessibility.
