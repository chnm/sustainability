        Marks of Distinction
        	Madisonville, Louisiana


        On the north bank of Lake Pontchartrain
        where the lake and the Techfunctie merge
        a squat, lighthouse sits
        ghostly
        and forgotten.

        Pelicans perch
        on giant wood pilings,
        a dock’s skeletal remains.

        A tugboat lays pitched on its side
        half hidden by brackish water,
        abandoned to crabs and weeds.

        1077 runs a mile from lighthouse to town
        a pitted concrete single-car bridge
        widens to a stiltsville of houses.

        Waterfront restaurants survive
        show off waterlines
        fifteen feet high.

        Madisonville, etched by Katrina.

        ~Linda Joyce
