description: Williamson writes of his son's fever, and says that he would like to put the boys in school, somewhere near him, which he cannot do in the city due to finances. He has heard that there is to
  be a position as surveyor general in North Carolina, and he is hopeful that McHenry could mention to the President his interest in this position, as well as his knowledge of geometry. The surveyor is
  to run the line of boundary between the US and Spain for Florida. Williamson also doubts that France can restrain itself from another campaign.
