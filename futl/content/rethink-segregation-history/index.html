My Notes

What locations on the map do you recognize today? : I recognize Washington and Lee, but nothing else really.
What do you think the artist’s point of view in this drawing?:
The artist's point of view was just to mainly show that this was the first vote of many African Americans and that it was a major step towards equality.
What can this source tell us about the challenges faced by African Americans during segregation? :
Most whites did not question Jim Crow segregation because they probably just simply believed that was the way the world and their society should be like.
Why do you think this map was created? :
The map was created to show how many and or where lynchings were occurring all through the states.
What evidence leads you to believe that African Americans in Arlington felt pride in their school or accomplishments?:
The lines in which they say that they love their school, they have pride in their school and that they would protect their school if they have to.
Why do you think Mrs. Deskins didn’t tell her children about segregation?:
Mrs. Deskins didn't tell her kids about segregation because she felt like it may limit them and they may think a certain way, she wanted them to enjoy life ad have fun doing so and not inform them about the restrictions that were upon them in their society that they couldn't do anything about.
