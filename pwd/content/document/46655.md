134
Pittsburgh 16th May 1794
Sir,
I have recd your letter of the 10th Int. The money put into my hands by Lieut Camp Smith is in notes Payable to Caleb Swan or bearer consequently current notes, it is therefore unnecessary to apply to Mr. Smith for an exchange.
I have herewith enclosed Muster & Pay Rolls of the the detachment stationed at Wheeling the other detachments have not yet completed all their Rolls, as soon as the are put into my hands the shall be examined & paid.
I expect the cask with the Silver this day and have detained Lieut. Campble Smith to take charge of it.
Please to send by next post a Certificate of the time to which Andrew McCleary Dragoon in Capt. Boyers Company say Troop has been paid up to, in order to enable him to obtain a settlement when he joins the Troop
I am Sir
Your Obedt
Huml Servt
Isaac Craig 
Joseph Howell
Accountant war
