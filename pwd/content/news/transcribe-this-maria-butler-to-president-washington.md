<p><a href="/person.php?id=1991">Maria Butler</a>, the “relick” (widow) of General Richard Butler, <a href="/document.php?id=6060">wrote</a> to <a href="/person.php?id=33">President George Washington</a> late in 1791 to express her concern over the insecurity of the frontier at Pittsburgh and points west. Her husband had only recently been killed in a battle between the Western Confederacy of Indians and the United States which took place near what is today the border between Ohio and Indiana and his body was buried on the field. Although the area around Pittsburgh seemed dangerous to Mrs. Butler, she was determined to remain in the area with her children.</p>
