  at Springfield.  Asks Wadsworth to send accounts of all supplies rendered, that Knox may forward them to the comptroller.  Knox notes he is leaving for Philadelphia; directs Wadsworth to either prepare
  to deliver the remaining money in his hands or to send it to Knox there, "in the manner you propose respecting Mr. Greene's money" [presumably the late Nathanael Greene].  Mentions enclosed [bank?]note
  [not included] from Mr. Willcox for $102.50, the surplus amount of the clothing account out of monies received for that purpose.  Asks that Wadsworth notify him of the receipt of Willcox's note.
