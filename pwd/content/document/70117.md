description: Hamilton admits that if there was anything in his recent general order that imputes to the Secretary of War ignorance or inattention, it ought not to have been there. The letter to Captain
  Frye was, in Hamilton's view, irregular and should have been addressed to Major Hoops. It seems that McHenry has overlooked the inconsistency of what was done with his instructions and overlooking a fault
  is very different from not attending to it. And it is understood that the civil staff can often conduct the business of supplies without it passing through the medium of the Chief Military Officer.
