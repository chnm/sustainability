November 18, 2007 \r\n12 noon - 6:00pm\r\nOak Street at South Carrollton\r\n\r\nThe first annual New Orleans Po-Boy Preservation Festival was held on Sunday, November 18, 2007 from noon to 6:00pm.\r\n\r\nThe festival was held along the 8100-8300 blocks of Oak Street between S. Carrollton Ave and Cambronne Street.\r\n\r\nThe first annual New Orleans Po-Boy Preservation Festival featured po-boy offerings from some of the city\'s most famous restaurants.\r\n\r\nFestival Judges presented the \"Golden Loaf Awards\" for the best tasting po-boys. Judges included Tom Fitzmorris host of \"The Food Show\" on WWWL-AM.\r\n\r\nThe festival featured 2 stages with live music, arts &amp; crafts, a silent auction, a children\'s section with games &amp; prizes, a beer garden with a large screen TV to view the Saints as they play on their game day; and of course, the best po-boys in New Orleans.\r\n\r\n
