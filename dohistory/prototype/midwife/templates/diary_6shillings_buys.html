May 21, 1787

 2 Clear. the Girls Strikeing harnis. I went to See mrs Williams. mrs Voce & Betsy here. mr Smyle Sleeps here. mr Ballard surveying for Revd mr Foster & been to Doct Coneys. mrs Harris, Lidia Bisbee, Sally Peirce & others here ys Evinng. mr Savage made the irons for our Loome. I paid him 4 Shillings in Cash.



Aug 2, 1787

 5 Clear & Hott. Hannah washt. Clarisa Barton here. I have felt unwell, been at home all Day. Robins gon with a raft for Eljh Daves. mr Blake Sent me 6 lb of Chees at /6 pr lb.



Aug 3, 1792

 6 Clear. I returnd home, Calld at mr Pollards. Hannah informd me mrs Sheppard wisht to See me & I went, find her Cleverly. he made me a prest off a bottle off wine. I purchast a pair [ether Sliprs @ 5/, papr pins /9.
at Savages, Pollards & Sheppards.

June 19, 1809

 2 Clear part of the day. mr Ballard went to James Boltons, brot A Cow & Calf home. Wm Ballard helpt him. we had a fine Shower [afterorn]. I dug ground South of the hous to Sett Turnip plants. mr Ballard went to Doct Conys, Setled with him. Ballance in our favour 20 Cents for which he had 1 oz Bitters. ye Doct made a present of 1 oz Camphir.
 		at home, workt Some in my gardin. Doct Cony gave me 1 oz Camphir.

During the American Revolution, the Continental Congress issued dollars, based on the familiar Spanish silver piece of eight, also called a dollar. The revolutionary states also issued their own money, some in dollars and cents and some in pounds, shillings and pence.
