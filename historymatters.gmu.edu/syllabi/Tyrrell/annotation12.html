This is the topic (with the follow-up in WEEK 12) that most interests Australian students. They have an empathy with African-American music and other aspects of contemporary black culture, but more deeply they are interested in the paradoxes of slavery and freedom, and the complexities of human bondage. The very idea of one individual having apparently total control over another–they assume this–is fascinating to them, and of course they learn much from this about the ways in which American historiography shows slavery resistance and development of cultural identity. The comparisons and contrasts between convicts and Indians is a useful entrée into this topic, since Sydney was originally a British penal settlement.
