Found: evidence to answer the question, "Why did Martha record some transactions in shillings and pence and some in dollars and cents?"


Martha Ballard lived through political and economic turmoil swirling around the American Revolution. Much of her life, there was no unified currency that she and her neighbors used. As governments fell and rose, so did their currencies. Some old forms persisted while new forms came and went. Most of the time, cash was scarce. In the Hallowell credit economy, the people of Hallowell, Maine used mixed currencies but persisted in thinking in pounds, shillings, and pence. Years after the Revolution had ended, Martha Ballard still noted her fees and purchases in shillings and pence. Change came gradually. Only toward the end of the diary do we start to see references to U.S. dollars and cents.

This is an example where a private document, Martha Ballard's diary, tells us more about actual practice than public documents, such as legislative acts to coin money, tell us.
