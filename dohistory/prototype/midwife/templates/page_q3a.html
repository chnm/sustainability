Found: evidence indicating whether or not Dr. Page owned a copy of Smellie's Abridgement of the Practice of Midwifery.


Probate records (inventories taken to execute wills) are useful guides to the economic status and tastes of individuals. Dr. Page's probate record tells us that he owned four copies of William Smellie's Abridgement of the Practice of Midwifery. Smellie taught midwifery classes to male-midwives and doctors in England. His book was reprinted often and it was owned by most physicians in the US and England who worked in obstetrics.

The filmmakers of A Midwife's Tale frequently referred to probate records when designing the interiors of houses and although they were immensely useful in figuring out how many chairs, oxen, or tables a person owned they do not tell us what kind of chairs or about the health of the livestock. Consulting tax records, census records, letters of correspondence, and other court records can help narrow down the possibilities and, with a bit of imagination, allows one to make a good educated guess.
