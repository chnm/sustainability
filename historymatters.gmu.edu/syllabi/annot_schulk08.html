The simulation paper is a joint creative writing assignment which requires that the student try to resolve a historical problem and project the probable consequences of the chosen solution in the form of a short story, play, exchange of letters, series of diary entries or screen play. Papers are evaluated jointly by the history and English instructors on the basis of creativity, mechanics and historical verisimilitude. Each student is required to submit a first draft, which is read and commented upon by both instructors, and a fully revised, final draft. The subject of the second episode of the simulation on which this paper is based is the efforts of ordinary Americans to cope with rapid social and economic change during the 1830s.
