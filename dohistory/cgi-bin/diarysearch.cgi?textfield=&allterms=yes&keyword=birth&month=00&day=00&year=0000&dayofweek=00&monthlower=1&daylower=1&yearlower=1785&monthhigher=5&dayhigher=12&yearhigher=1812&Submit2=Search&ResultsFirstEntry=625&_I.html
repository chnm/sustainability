 October 9, 1790, Saturday

view full Diary page

 

X Birth. Jere Whites Son. XX.

Cloudy & rain foren. mrs White was Safe Deld of a Son at 10 h morn. I tarried with her till 4 pm then went home by water. feel much fatagud. was informd that mr Hamlin & Burjis had lost Linning.   at Dittos. Birth 26th. firsT Born.  
 

 

 October 12, 1790, Tuesday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear. mr Ballard Came from mr Pages, left his Spectacles; went Back for them. I was Calld to See Edsons infant who is Extreamly ill; tarried & watcht.   at mr Kennadys. 
 

 

 October 13, 1790, Wednesday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear. Doct Coney Came to See the Child, orderd Blisters & left Senna and some Drops. I Came from there at noon, left the Child very ill. Cyrus there near night. the Neighbours there, it was thot to be Expireing.   at Dittoes. Dolly & Hannah Rockwood here.  
 

 

 October 14, 1790, Thursday

view full Diary page

 

 rainy. mr Chamberlin Calld me to See a wound his Dagt Polly had on her right Legg. I Dresst it with lint and Spirrit. Wm Haywood & Polly Bisbe here. my Girls went with them to mr Burtuns. mr Ballard been to ye hook to a propriators meeting. I was Calld in great hast to See mrs Hamlin who was in a fitt. I walkt there, applyd Vinagar to her Lips, temples & hands & Onions to her feet & Shee revivd. I tarried all night. mrs Edsons inft Expird Last night.   at mr Chamln and was at [    ] to See mrs Hamlin who had a fitt. Death.  
 

 

 October 21, 1790, Thursday

view full Diary page

 

 I returnd. Calld at mr Blacks and Dind. was Calld at 9h Evn to mr Snells, his wife in Travil. mr Winslow attended me, arivd there before 11.   at Ditoes & mr Snells.  
 

 

 October 22, 1790, Friday

view full Diary page

 

X Birth. Snells Dagt. XX.

Cloudy Some part ye Day. I was with mrs Snells who was very ill thro the Day and was Safe Delivd at 9h Evn of a Dagt, her first Child. mrs Cox, Bullin, Blanchard, Brown and mrs Winslow attendants.   at mr Snells. Birth 27th. 
 

 

 October 23, 1790, Saturday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear & very pleast. I Left mrs Snell & infant Cleverly; receivd 6/ as reward. Calld at Capt Husseys, he made me a present of a waistcoat for my little Son. I Calld at Colo Sewalls & Colo Howards. mrs Polly is better. got home at noone, finisht a pair of mitts for mr Ballard.   at Dittoes. returnd home.  
 

 

 November 8, 1790, Monday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear. the Girls quilted a Bed quillt. & went to mr Craggs; Spent Evng. I went to mr Westons, Bot 3dz needles, 1-1/2 lb Sugar. Calld at mr Craggs & Savages.   at mr Westons. Polly Bisbe here.  
 

 

 November 10, 1790, Wednesday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear. my Girls had Some Neighbours to help ym quilt a Bed quilt. 15tn Ladies, they began to quillt at 3h pm, finisht & took it out at y Evn. there were 12 Gentlemen took Tea. they Danced a little while after Supper, behavd Exceeding Cleverly; were all returnd home before ye 11th h.   at home. we had Company.  
 

 

 November 12, 1790, Friday

view full Diary page

 

 a Cloudy day. I was Calld by Colo Sewall to See his Lady who was in Labour. Shee was not So ill as to Call in other assistance this Day. I Slept with her till about 1h morn when Shee Calld her Neighbours to her assistance.   at Colo Sewalls.  
 

 

 November 13, 1790, Saturday

view full Diary page

 

X Birth. Colo Sewalls Son. XX.

A Clear & Butifull Day. mrs Sewall was ill till 3 h pm when Shee was thro Divine asistance made the Living Mother of a Living Son, her 3rd Child. mrs Brooks, Belcher, Colman, Pollard & Voce assisted us. I went to Colo Howard at Evng to ask assistance for Polly Taytor. Colo Sewall gave me 6/8 as a reward, Conducted me over ye river. I Calld at mr Craggs, was informd that Hannah North was thot to be Expireing this morn but is revived. mrs Colman informd me that the Doct, her Husband, gave Hannah a Dose of Callom Calomel for worms which gave relief. She went next Day to See mrs Goodin and took Cold, which has brot her into the Sittuation Shee now is. when I arivd home found Capt Silva Learned here from oxford. Letters.   at Dittoes. Capt Silva Learned of Oxford Sleeps here, brot a letter fm Broth Collins, 1 fm. Sister Barton of Oct. 31. Jon a killd his Oxen. Birth 28th. 
 

 

 November 23, 1790, Tuesday

view full Diary page

 

 rainy. Seth Foster workt here, moovd fence on ye point South Side ye Crick. mr Putnam Calld and informd me mr Ballard was well last Satturday and yt mr Gillman was Some unwell. I have been at home.   at home. Dolly made her Callamanco Skirt. Death, Jery Whites inft. 
 

 

 November 26, 1790, Friday

view full Diary page

 

X Birth. Ezra Hodges 3d dagt & 5th Child; the first was 5 ys old ye 10 of Sept last. XX

10 of Sept last. XX Clear part of the Day & very Cold. I am informd that Jona & others who were attempting to go Down with a rafft were inclosd by the ice at Bumber hook Point. thee ice makes very fast. Doct Williams & his Son here at Evn. Could not pass with yr Horses, left ym here and went to mr Pollards. I was Calld at 8 h Evn to See mrs Hodges in Labour. her women were Calld and Shee was Safe Delivrd at 10 h and 30 m Evn. mrs Pollard being unwell Did not arive till the moment the Child was Born. the Ladies who assisted took Supper after all our matters were Completed, & retend home about 12. mrs Pollard and my Self tarried all night & Sett up. mrs Hodges rested finely, ye inft allso.   at mr Savages and mr Hodges. thee ice Stopt at Fort Westorn. Birth 29th. Saml Dawin fell thro ye ice as he was attempting to go of ye rafft to ye Shore, but was Savd by their geting to him with a Cannoe & takeing him in.  
 

 

 November 30, 1790, Tuesday

view full Diary page

 

X Ephm Cowens Son Born. XX.

Clear. I was Calld at [ye 7th] h morn to Ephm Cowens wife in Labour. Shee was Deld at 9 Evn of a Son. I tarried all night.   at Ephm Cowens. Birth 30.  
 

 

 December 1, 1790, Wednesday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear & Pleasant. I Left my patients Cleverly and returnd at 11 hours morn. mr Cowen let me have 3/4 lb Tea and 1 lb Sugar. I began to foot a pair of hoes for mrs Pollard. Set up Late to work.   at mr Cowens.  
 

 

 December 3, 1790, Friday

view full Diary page

 

X Birth. Asa Williamss Dagt. XX.

A Cold Snowy Day. I was Calld at ye 6th hour morn to See mrs Williams. Shee was Safe Delivrd of her 4th Dagt at 1 h Pm, and I returnd home at ye 6th Evn. found mr Ballard returnd from his tour of Surveying. I began to foot a pair of hoes for mrs Pollard. I receivd 6/ as a reward.   at Asa Williamss. Birth 31st. A Snow.  
 

 

 December 6, 1790, Monday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear and Cold. mrs H. Thomson here; informs me mrs Williams is Cleverly. I have been at home; knitt a pair of mitts for Ephm. Ephm Cowen Sent me 3/4 lb Tea.   at home. Parthena gone to work for mrs Burtun.  
 

 

 December 14, 1790, Tuesday

view full Diary page

 

 Over cast part of the day, and moderate. I have been at home mending hoes and knitting. Dolly had a faint turn this Eving, and pain in her Side. I gave her Caster.   I was at home. Birth D Fletchers Dagt.  
 

 

 December 18, 1790, Saturday

view full Diary page

 

X Birth. Benn Browns Dagt. XX. receivd my fee Novr 22nt 1791 in Turkeys.

Clear and very Cold. Dolly is unwell, took Soap pills, had Several faint turns. I went to See Polly mc kethney, find her very low in Strength and Spirrits. Doct Colman let blood in both her feet. I was Calld at ye 8th h Evn in hast to See mrs Brown. Shee was Safe Deld at 9 h 30 m of a fine Dagt, her 7th Child. mr Brown was absent. mrs Woodwd and my self tarried all night.   at mr Craggs & Browns. Birth 32nt. Death Polly Taylor.  
 

 

 December 25, 1790, Saturday

view full Diary page

 

 Cloudy & Cold. mr Cragg Calld me to See Polly mc kethney. Shee is no Better. mrs Voce Came and we Changd her linning. I find Dolly very ill at my return, her Blister very painfull. mr Putnam of 7 mile Brook took Break fast here.   at Does. Snowd all night.  
 

 

 December 27, 1790, Monday

view full Diary page

 

 Clear. mr Cragg Calld me to See Polly. Shee Seems Better in Some respects, but her blister on ye Left arm Looks very black. I helpt remove her from one bed to another and returnd. Dolly was very Faint indeed till near night, when Shee Seemed much more Comfortable, and Sett up ye Evng. I was Calld at 10 h Evn to See thee wife of Shuball Wiesom. met with diffiCulty on ye way beyond mr Burjeses. Came back to his house, he went with us. I arivd Safe, tarried all night.   at mr Craggs and Wiesoms.  
 

 

 December 28, 1790, Tuesday

view full Diary page

 

Birth. Capt Burjess Dagt.

Clear and very Cold. mr Burjes Calld me from Wiesoms before Sun rise to his wife in travil. Shee was Safe Deld of a Dagt at Sun Sett. I tarried all night, her Nece was unwell, her Little Son allso.   at [Capt] Burjeses. Birth 33.  
 

 

 December 29, 1790, Wednesday

view full Diary page

 

 The Sun rose Clear, Snowd Soon after. I left my patients more Comfortable. Calld to See Sally Hamlin & Hannah Cragg; find ym Better. Polly mc kethney Seems a little Better. arivd at home by 10 morn, find mr Porter and wife here. Dolly is Better. Hannahs face is not so badly Scolt as we Expected. Shee Spilt Boiling Licour on her foot this Day and Scolt it Some. Jane Welch Came for me to go and See her Mother. I went. her Complaints are pain in her head and limbs. mr Porter and wife & Hannah went to mr Burtuns.   at Ditoes. Hamlins & mr Craggs & mrs Walches. mrs Porter gave me 1/2 lb of wool.  
 

 

 December 30, 1790, Thursday

view full Diary page

 

X Birth. Shubal Wiesoms Son. XX.

Clear and very Cold indeed. I was Calld at ye Dawn of Day to Wiesoms, found his wife Suffering for want of help. Shee was Deld of a Son ye 8th h. the inft I was feirfull wd Expire but revivd and I left mother & Child Cleverly. arivd at home at 12/ O Clok, then went to mr Burjeses. I gave his wife Some medisin, walkt home. Calld to See Polly mc kethney who it is that is mending slowly   at Wiesoms. Birth 34.  
 

 

 January 4, 1791, Tuesday

view full Diary page

 

 Cloudy, the weather moderate. Doct Coney here; took acount of Births & Deaths ye year past from my minnits. I have been at home.   at home.
